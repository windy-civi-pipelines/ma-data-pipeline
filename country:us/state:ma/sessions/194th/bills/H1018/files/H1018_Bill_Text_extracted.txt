By Representative Peisch of Wellesley, a petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 1018) of
Alice Hanlon Peisch relative to water resources and protecting the rights of municipalities and
districts. Environment and Natural Resources.
[SIMILAR MATTER FILED IN PREVIOUS SESSION
SEE HOUSE, NO. 879 OF 2023-2024.]
The Commonwealth of Massachusetts
_______________
In the One Hundred and Ninety-Fourth General Court
(2025-2026)
_______________
An Act updating the management of the Commonwealth's water resources.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court assembled, and by the authority
of the same, as follows:
1 SECTION 1. Section 2 of chapter 21G of the General Laws, as appearing in the 2022
2 Official Edition, is hereby amended by inserting at the end of the definition of “Permit” the
3 following sentence:-
4 The permit program is intended to assist the department in the comprehensive
5 management of the commonwealth’s water resources within its river basins in a manner that
6 ensures an appropriate balance among competing water withdrawals and uses, as well as
7 preservation of the water resource itself.
1 of 2
8 SECTION 2. Said section 2 of said chapter 21G of the General Laws, as so appearing, is
9 hereby further amended by inserting at the end of the definition of “Registration statement” the
10 following sentence:-
11 The registration statement is intended to enable the department to document baseline
12 water use in the commonwealth.
13 SECTION 3. Section 5 of said chapter 21G of the General Laws, as appearing in the 2022
14 Official Edition, is hereby amended by adding the following paragraph:-
15 All properly filed water management act renewal registration statements shall entitle the
16 registrants to their registered water use volumes without conditions excepting that the department
17 may require proper metering or other means to accurately measure the volume of water
18 withdrawn under a renewal registration statement. No regulations may be promulgated that
19 would allow the imposition of any such conditions other than withdrawal volume measurement.
2 of 2
