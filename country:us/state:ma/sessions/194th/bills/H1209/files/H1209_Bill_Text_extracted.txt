HOUSE DOCKET, NO. 3374 FILED ON: 1/17/2025
HOUSE . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . No. 1209
By Representative Jones of North Reading, a petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 1209) of
Bradley H. Jones, Jr., and others for an investigation and study by a special commission
(including members of the General Court) to study unemployment insurance reform. Financial
Services.
The Commonwealth of Massachusetts
_______________
In the One Hundred and Ninety-Fourth General Court
(2025-2026)
_______________
An Act creating a commission to study unemployment insurance reform.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court assembled, and by the authority
of the same, as follows:
1 SECTION 1. There shall be a special commission to: (i) evaluate the Commonwealth’s
2 current unemployment policies and assess their effect on the health and growth of Massachusetts
3 small businesses, and (ii) make recommendations to improve the existing unemployment
4 insurance procedures in regard to fostering economic growth and incentivizing employers to hire
5 additional workers. The commission shall file its findings and recommendations, together with
6 drafts of legislation necessary to carry those recommendations into effect, with the clerks of the
7 senate and the house of representatives not later than December 31, 2027.
8 The commission shall consist of 1 member who shall be appointed by the speaker of the
9 house of representatives; 1 member who shall be appointed by the minority leader of the house
10 of representatives; 1 member who shall be appointed by the senate president; 1 member who
11 shall be appointed by the minority leader of the senate; the secretary of labor and workforce
12 development or a designee; the secretary of economic development or a designee; the chair of
1 of 2
13 the joint committee on community development and small business; the chair of the joint
14 committee on labor and workforce development; the chair of the joint committee on economic
15 development and emerging technologies; and 1 member to be appointed by each of the following
16 organizations: Associated Industries of Massachusetts, the Massachusetts Restaurant
17 Association, the Massachusetts chapter of the National Federation of Independent Businesses,
18 and the Massachusetts State Labor Council.
19
