HOUSE DOCKET, NO. 3750 FILED ON: 1/17/2025
HOUSE . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . No. 1210
By Representative Jones of North Reading, a petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 1210) of
Bradley H. Jones, Jr., and others relative to artificial intelligence health communications and
informed patient consent. Financial Services.
The Commonwealth of Massachusetts
_______________
In the One Hundred and Ninety-Fourth General Court
(2025-2026)
_______________
An Act relative to AI health communications and informed patient consent.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court assembled, and by the authority
of the same, as follows:
1 SECTION 1. Section 70E of Chapter 111 of the General Laws, as appearing in the 2022
2 Official Edition, is hereby amended by striking out, in line 46, subsection (b), and inserting in
3 place thereof the following language;
4 (b) to confidentiality of all records and communications to the extent provided by law;
5 (b ½) to be informed, if the information they are receiving either verbally or in writing
6 has been generated by artificial intelligence, and to be provided with instructions about how to
7 contact a human healthcare provider in the event that such information was not previously
8 reviewed and approved by their provider;
9 SECTION 2. Section 6 of Chapter 176O of the General Laws, as appearing in the 2022
10 Official Edition, is hereby amended by inserting after subsection (9) the following:
1 of 2
11 (9 ½) a disclosure if artificial intelligence algorithms or automated decision tools are
12 being utilized or will be utilized in the claims review process, such a disclosure must include a
13 summary of what tools are being used and how they are being used throughout the claims review
14 process;
15 SECTION 3. Section 12 of Chapter 176O of the General Laws, as appearing in the 2022
16 Official Edition, is hereby amended by inserting the following subsection:
17 (g) A carrier shall submit to the division of insurance, no later than December 31 each
18 year, a form to be prescribed by the division, which shall detail the artificial intelligence
19 algorithms and data training sets that are currently being used or will be used in the utilization
20 review process by the carrier. A carrier shall also submit an attestation, in a manner and form
21 prescribed by the division, that such algorithms and training data sets have minimized the risk of
22 bias based on the covered person's race, color, religious creed, ancestry, age, sex, gender,
23 national origin, handicap or disability, and adhere to evidence-based clinical guidelines.
