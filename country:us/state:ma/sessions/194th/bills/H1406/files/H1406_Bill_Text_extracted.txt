HOUSE DOCKET, NO. 2860 FILED ON: 1/16/2025
HOUSE . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . No. 1406
By Representative Sabadosa of Northampton, a petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 1406)
of Lindsay N. Sabadosa relative to continuity of care for MassHealth members with chronic
conditions. Health Care Financing.
The Commonwealth of Massachusetts
_______________
In the One Hundred and Ninety-Fourth General Court
(2025-2026)
_______________
An Act to improve continuity of care for MassHealth members with chronic conditions.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court assembled, and by the authority
of the same, as follows:
1 Section 1. Chapter 118E of the General Laws is hereby amended by adding, before
2 Chapter D, the following new section:
3 Section 9C1/2. Prior Authorization Requirements Related to Community-based Long-
4 term Care for MassHealth Members with Chronic Conditions.
5 (1) For purposes of this section, the following terms shall have the following definitions:
6 “Community long-term care services” shall include, but is not limited to: Adult Day
7 Health, Adult Foster Care, Group Adult Foster Care, Day Habilitation, Home Health Aide,
8 Personal Care Attendant Services, and all services described in the Frail Elder Waiver that was in
9 effect as of April 1, 2024, provided that these long-term care services include services itemized
10 in the Frail Elder Waiver as well as said services included in any other 1915(c) waiver in effect
11 as of April 1, 2024;
1 of 2
12 “Chronic condition” is a health condition or disease that is persistent or otherwise
13 expected to last longer than one year; include intellectual or developmental disabilities.
14 (2) Where the agency requires prior authorization for long-term services and supports,
15 prior authorization for long-term services and supports that address a chronic condition shall
16 issue for the duration of the prescribed or ordered service for a prior authorization period of up to
17 five (5) years.
18 Nothing in this section shall preclude MassHealth or its authorized agent from modifying
19 a service plan at the request of the member or a primary care provider in response to a change in
20 a member’s condition and their corresponding service need.
21 Section 2. Section 4B of Chapter 19A of the General Laws is hereby amended by adding,
22 after the fourth paragraph, the following paragraph:
23 Regarding the service authorization duties enabled by paragraph one in this section,
24 where a skilled medical provider employed by an ASAP conducts screening activities and
25 service authorizations for community long-term care services for which the ASAP will not be the
26 direct service provider, the service authorization shall constitute a prior authorization for
27 community long-term care services. ASAPs shall work to promote the prompt delivery of
28 services pursuant to a service plan. The provisions of this paragraph shall apply to the senior care
29 options initiative established by section 9D of Chapter 118E. Nothing in this section shall
30 preclude MassHealth or its authorized agent from modifying a service plan at the direction of the
31 member or a primary care provider in response to a change in a member’s condition and their
32 corresponding service need.
2 of 2
