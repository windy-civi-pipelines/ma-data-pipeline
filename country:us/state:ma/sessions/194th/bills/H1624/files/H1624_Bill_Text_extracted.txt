HOUSE DOCKET, NO. 4243 FILED ON: 1/17/2025
HOUSE . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . No. 1624
By Representative Connolly of Cambridge, a petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 1624) of
Mike Connolly for legislation to establish an interagency task force to study the public health
and social justice implications of legalizing the possession, consumption, transportation, and
distribution of naturally cultivated entheogenic plants and fungi. The Judiciary.
[SIMILAR MATTER FILED IN PREVIOUS SESSION
SEE HOUSE, NO. 1494 OF 2021-2022.]
The Commonwealth of Massachusetts
_______________
In the One Hundred and Ninety-Fourth General Court
(2025-2026)
_______________
An Act establishing a task force to study equitable access to psilocybin and other entheogenic
plants.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court assembled, and by the authority
of the same, as follows:
1 There shall be an interagency task force to study the public health and social justice
2 implications of legalizing the possession, consumption, transportation, and distribution of
3 psilocybin and other naturally cultivated entheogenic plants and fungi. The task force shall
4 consist of 21 members: 1 member shall be appointed by the senate president, who shall serve as
5 co-chair; 1 member who shall be appointed by the minority leader of the senate; 1 member who
6 shall be appointed by the speaker of the house of representatives, who shall serve as co-chair; 1
7 member who shall be appointed by the minority leader of the house of representatives; 1 member
8 of the Board of Allied Mental Health and Human Services Professions or its agreed upon
9 designee; 1 member of the Cannabis Control Commission; 2 members with professional
1 of 3
10 advocacy or academic experience with drug-related harm reduction policies and methods, at least
11 1 of whom should be a practitioner of traditional, ceremonial plant medicine practices; 3
12 members with training in medicine, social work, public health, or law and expertise in addressing
13 health disparities and access to care; 4 members with professional, advocacy, or academic
14 experience promoting racial equity and economic justice, at least 1 of whom shall be a
15 representative of a Massachusetts recognized Native American tribe; 2 members with
16 professional scientific expertise in the physiological and psychological effects of the full
17 spectrum of entheogenic plants, fungi, and natural materials containing indole amines,
18 tryptamines, phenethylamines, including psilocybin, ayahuasca, cacti, and iboga; the attorney
19 general of Massachusetts or their designee; the secretary of the executive office of health and
20 human services or their designee; the secretary of the department of veterans’ services or their
21 designee; the secretary of the executive office of public safety and security or their designee.
22 The task force shall: (i) compile and review research regarding the physiological and
23 psychological effects of entheogenic plants and fungi; (ii) compile testimony and data on the
24 experiences of communities across the United States—including Somerville (MA), Cambridge
25 (MA), Denver (CO), Oakland (CA), Santa Cruz (CA), Ann Arbor (MI), sovereign native lands,
26 Washington D.C. and Oregon—as well as international jurisdictions that pursued harm reduction
27 strategies with respect to entheogens—including Portugal, the Netherlands, Jamaica, Peru, and
28 Brazil; (iii) compile and review research regarding the impact of controlled substances
29 prohibition on marginalized groups, including indigenous people, veterans, people with physical
30 and mental health disabilities, Black people, people of Latino and Hispanic heritage, people of
31 Asian descent, people of color, people in poverty, and people identifying with the LGBTQ
32 community; (iv) compile and review research on the political and cultural origins of prohibition
2 of 3
33 policies for controlled substances; (v) develop recommendations for legalizing and eliminating
34 Massachusetts state penalties for possession, consumption, transportation, manufacture, and
35 distribution of entheogenic plants and fungi in a manner that maximizes equitable access and
36 sustainable manufacture of these plants; (vi) identify opportunities for public education on harm-
37 reduction for controlled substances, including revisions to policies related to the licensing of
38 physical and mental healthcare workers and therapists across the Commonwealth; (vii) develop
39 recommendations regarding pardons, parole, diversion, expungement, and equity measures for
40 people with criminal records related to possession, consumption, and distribution of entheogenic
41 plants and fungi and other controlled substances; (viii) hear and consider public testimony by
42 Massachusetts residents who have used entheogenic plants and fungi and/or endured experiences
43 related to incarceration for controlled substances possession.
44 The task force shall file a report of its findings and recommendations, together with drafts
45 of legislation necessary to carry those recommendations into effect, by filing the same with the
46 clerks of the senate and the house of representatives, the chairs of the senate and house
47 committees on ways and means, the senate and house chairs of the joint committee on public
48 health, the senate and house chairs of the joint committee on the judiciary, the senate and house
49 chairs of the joint committee on public safety and homeland security not later than June 2026.
