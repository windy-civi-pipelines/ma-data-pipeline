HOUSE DOCKET, NO. 2968 FILED ON: 1/16/2025
HOUSE . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . No. 1981
By Representative Sabadosa of Northampton, a petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 1981)
of Lindsay N. Sabadosa and others relative to discrimination for wearing necessary personal
protective medical equipment. The Judiciary.
The Commonwealth of Massachusetts
_______________
In the One Hundred and Ninety-Fourth General Court
(2025-2026)
_______________
An Act relative to the right to wear personal protective medical equipment.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court assembled, and by the authority
of the same, as follows:
1 SECTION 1. The following, as used in this chapter, unless the text otherwise requires or
2 a different meaning is specifically required, shall mean—
3 ‘Protective medical equipment,’ equipment that is worn to minimize exposure to health
4 risks, which includes, but is not limited to, face masks, respirators, gloves, face shields,
5 protective eyewear, and gowns.
6 SECTION 2. (a) No individual shall be discriminated against or penalized for wearing
7 necessary personal protective medical equipment.
8 (b) Discrimination includes, but is not limited to, denial of service, eviction from
9 premises, termination of employment or undue pressure to refrain from wearing personal
10 protective medical equipment.
1 of 2
11 SECTION 3. The request to momentarily remove personal protective medical equipment
12 is acceptable at security checkpoints and at the request of law enforcement for identification
13 purposes.
2 of 2
