196 Section 4. The department shall coordinate matters that impact people with an alcohol use
197 disorder in the commonwealth, shall establish and conduct programs for the treatment,

199 adults, in cooperation with public and private agencies, business and industry, and shall provide
200 technical assistance and consultation services whenever required. The department may adopt
201 rules and regulations to carry out its powers and duties under this chapter. The department shall
202 be responsible for assuring that the following services are available and shall utilize community
203 mental health centers and existing facilities and services available within the private sector
204 whenever possible: (1) withdrawal management services on a twenty-four hour basis in or near
205 population centers which meet the immediate medical and physical needs of people with an
206 alcohol use disorder, including necessary diagnostic and referral services; (2) medical and
207 hospital services which are available on a twenty-four hour basis and which, whenever possible,
208 are provided in existing general hospital facilities; (3) rehabilitation services, including family
209 care, residential aftercare and appropriate therapy which, whenever possible, shall be coordinated
210 with MassAbility; (4) inpatient psychiatric services for individuals with co-occurring mental
211 health and substance use disorders which, whenever possible, shall be conducted with services
212 available in the department of mental health; and (5) training programs for professional and
213 nonprofessional workers in the alcohol use disorder treatment field. The department shall not
214 grant its approval for the establishment or maintenance of a facility, pursuant to section seven, in
215 any jail, house of correction or institution operated by the department of correction; provided,
