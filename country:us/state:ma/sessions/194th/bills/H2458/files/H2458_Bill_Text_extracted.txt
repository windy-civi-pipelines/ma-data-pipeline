HOUSE DOCKET, NO. 3737 FILED ON: 1/17/2025
HOUSE . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . No. 2458
By Representative Jones of North Reading, a petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 2458) of
Bradley H. Jones, Jr. and others for an investigation by a special commission (including
members of the General Court) relative to the promotion of direct primary care services. Public
Health.
The Commonwealth of Massachusetts
_______________
In the One Hundred and Ninety-Fourth General Court
(2025-2026)
_______________
An Act establishing a commission to study the promotion of direct primary care services.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court assembled, and by the authority
of the same, as follows:
1 SECTION 1. Notwithstanding any general or special law to the contrary, there shall be a
2 commission to study the benefits of direct primary care practices, and how these services can be
3 promoted in Massachusetts to ease the burden on the state's healthcare system and promote
4 access to care.
5 SECTION 2. The commission shall study and report on the benefits of direct primary
6 care services, including, but not limited to, reduced health care and prescription drug costs, cost
7 transparency, enhanced access to care, and improved health outcomes. The commission’s report
8 shall include strategies for promoting direct primary care services in the commonwealth,
9 including any policy recommendations to expand access to and increase public awareness of
10 these services.
1 of 2
11 SECTION 3. The commission shall consist of the secretary of the executive office of
12 health & human services or their designee, who shall serve as the chair; the house and senate
13 chairs of the joint committee on public health; the house and senate chairs of the joint committee
14 on healthcare financing; the CEO of the Massachusetts Health and Hospital Association, or their
15 designee; 2 representatives from the direct primary care industry to be appointed by the secretary
16 of the executive office of health & human services; 1 member to be appointed by the minority
17 leader of the house; and 1 member to be appointed by the minority leader of the senate.
18 SECTION 4. The commission shall hold no fewer than 3 public hearings to gather input
19 from stakeholders, including, but not limited to, representatives of hospital and health systems,
20 direct primary care physicians, and patient advocacy organizations.
21 SECTION 5. The commission shall file its report, including findings and
22 recommendations, with the clerks of the house of representatives and senate, the joint committee
23 on public health, and the joint committee on healthcare financing not later than 12 months after
24 the effective date of this act.
25 SECTION 6. This act shall take effect immediately upon passage.
2 of 2
