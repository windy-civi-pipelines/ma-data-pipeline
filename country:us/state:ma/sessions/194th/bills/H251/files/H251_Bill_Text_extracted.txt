HOUSE DOCKET, NO. 3752 FILED ON: 1/17/2025
HOUSE . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . No. 251
By Representative Jones of North Reading, a petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 251) of
Bradley H. Jones, Jr., and others that the Office of the State Auditor annually conduct an audit of
and report on the Department of Transitional Assistance. Children, Families and Persons with
Disabilities.
The Commonwealth of Massachusetts
_______________
In the One Hundred and Ninety-Fourth General Court
(2025-2026)
_______________
An Act requiring an audit to review the utilization of welfare benefits out-of-state.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court assembled, and by the authority
of the same, as follows:
1 SECTION 1. Notwithstanding any general or special law to the contrary, the office of the
2 state auditor shall conduct an annual audit of the department of transitional assistance. The scope
3 of the audit shall include a review of the following information from the previous fiscal year: the
4 processes and internal controls that the department uses to verify and issue benefits to eligible
5 recipients; how many individuals currently hold EBT cards; how many of those with cards are
6 actively receiving benefits; the total amount of benefits that have been utilized out-of-state; how
7 frequently benefits are used out-of-state; a list of locations where benefits are being spent out of
8 state; and a breakdown of the demographic information of those receiving benefits.
9 SECTION 2. The office of the state auditor shall submit an annual report with its findings
10 no later than January 1st each year, including any recommendations for how the department of
11 transitional assistance can improve its processes to help detect and reduce potential recipient and
12 retailer fraud, waste, or abuse, including improper use of benefits out-of-state. Additionally, the
1 of 2
13 report shall examine factors that may be driving increased enrollment for benefits, and what
14 projected fiscal and operational challenges the department of transitional assistance may face as
15 a result.
