HOUSE DOCKET, NO. 781 FILED ON: 1/13/2025
HOUSE . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . No. 2516
By Representative Ramos of Springfield, a petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 2516) of
Orlando Ramos and Jacob R. Oliveira relative to establishing an advisory committee (including
members of the General Court) on the accessibility of fresh food retail in communities. Public
Health.
[SIMILAR MATTER FILED IN PREVIOUS SESSION
SEE HOUSE, NO. 3632 OF 2023-2024.]
The Commonwealth of Massachusetts
_______________
In the One Hundred and Ninety-Fourth General Court
(2025-2026)
_______________
An Act establishing an advisory committee on the accessibility of fresh food retail in
communities throughout the Commonwealth.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court assembled, and by the authority
of the same, as follows:
1 SECTION 1. (a) There shall be an advisory committee to examine the accessibility of
2 fresh food retail in communities throughout the commonwealth. The advisory committee shall:
3 (i) develop criteria to determine which communities lack adequate access to fresh food retail,
4 particularly urban and rural communities; (ii) investigate the causes of inadequate access to fresh
5 food retail; (iii) study the public health impact of inadequate access to fresh food retail; (iv)
6 develop innovative strategies and recommendations to increase access to fresh food retail,
7 including: legislation, economic incentives and financing options, improvements to
8 transportation, public-private partnerships, pilot programs and the improvement of food
9 programs already in existence; and (v) calculate the costs and potential savings of all strategies
1 of 2
10 and recommendations and determine the benefits, drawbacks and feasibility of all strategies and
11 recommendations.
12 (b) The advisory committee shall consist of: (i) the commissioner of public health, or a
13 designee, who shall serve as chair of the advisory committee; (ii) the secretary of housing and
14 economic development, or a designee; (iii) the commissioner of agricultural resources, or a
15 designee; (iv) the chairs of the joint committee on public health; (v) a nutritionist appointed by
16 the commissioner of public health; and (vi) 5 members to be appointed by the governor, 1 of
17 whom shall be a representative of the supermarket industry, 1 of whom shall be a representative
18 of farmers markets, 1 of whom shall be a representative of the gateway cities, 1 of whom shall be
19 a representative of nonprofit and not-for-profit hunger relief organizations and 1 of whom shall
20 be a representative of the Massachusetts Public Health Association.
21 (c) The advisory committee shall commence no later than 90 days after the effective date
22 of this act.
23 (d) The advisory committee shall file a report with its findings, recommendations and
24 draft legislation to the senate and house committees on ways and means, the chairs of the joint
25 committee on community development and small business, the chairs of the joint committee on
26 public health and the chairs of the joint committee on children, families and persons with
27 disabilities no later than 18 months after enactment of this section.
28 SECTION 2. The advisory committee shall expire upon submission of the report pursuant
29 to subsection (d) of section 1.
