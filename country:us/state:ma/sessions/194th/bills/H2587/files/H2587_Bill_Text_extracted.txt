HOUSE DOCKET, NO. 2765 FILED ON: 1/16/2025
HOUSE . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . No. 2587
By Representative Decker of Cambridge, a petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 2587) of
Marjorie C. Decker for an investigation by a special commission (including members of the
General Court) relative to municipal emergency medical service staffing levels and workforce
development.. Public Safety and Homeland Security.
The Commonwealth of Massachusetts
_______________
In the One Hundred and Ninety-Fourth General Court
(2025-2026)
_______________
An Act establishing a commission on municipal emergency medical service staffing levels and
workforce development.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court assembled, and by the authority
of the same, as follows:
1 SECTION 1. (a) Notwithstanding any general or special law to the contrary, there shall
2 be a commission to study municipal emergency medical service staffing levels and workforce
3 development.
4 (b) The commission shall consist of 15 members: the chairs of the joint committee on
5 public safety and homeland security or their designees, who shall serve as co-chairs; the chairs of
6 the joint committee on public service or their designees; the state fire marshal or a designee; the
7 director of the office of emergency medical services or a designee; 2 members appointed by the
8 Professional Fire Fighters of Massachusetts; 2 members appointed by the Fire Chiefs Association
9 of Massachusetts; 2 members appointed by the Massachusetts Health and Hospital Association,
10 Inc; 1 member representing the Massachusetts Health Care Workforce Center; 1 member
1 of 2
11 representing the State Office of Rural Health; and 1 member appointed by the Massachusetts
12 Municipal Association.
13 (c) The commission shall: (i) evaluate the current effectiveness and efficiency of
14 municipal-based emergency medical service staffing, including an assessment of call volume,
15 response times, ambulance transport times, turnaround times for ambulances at hospitals, and
16 wait times for ambulances at hospitals; (ii) explore the current challenges facing the emergency
17 medical service workforce, including operational inefficiencies, staffing shortages, training
18 needs, and retention; (iii) review current emergency medical service and fire service workforce
19 development programs; and (iv) recommend changes to streamline the workforce, enhance
20 service delivery, and set standard staffing levels for services, as well as identify best practices in
21 workforce development to promote new member engagement, retention, and training.
22 (d) The commission shall submit a report of its study and recommendations, together
23 with any proposed legislation, to the chairs of the joint committee on public safety and homeland
24 security and the clerks of the house of representatives and senate no later than March 1, 2026.
