HOUSE DOCKET, NO. 1488 FILED ON: 1/15/2025
HOUSE . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . No. 2633
By Representative Higgins of Leominster, a petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 2633) of
Natalie M. Higgins and Michael O. Moore that the State 911 Department publish on its website a
statewide disability indicator form. Public Safety and Homeland Security.
[SIMILAR MATTER FILED IN PREVIOUS SESSION
SEE HOUSE, NO. 2343 OF 2023-2024.]
The Commonwealth of Massachusetts
_______________
In the One Hundred and Ninety-Fourth General Court
(2025-2026)
_______________
An Act relative to more accessible 911 disability indicator forms.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court assembled, and by the authority
of the same, as follows:
1 Chapter 6A of the General Laws is hereby amended by inserting after section 18D the
2 following section:-
3 Section 18D½. (a) The department shall create, in consultation with organizations
4 representing the mobility, hearing, speech and sight impaired communities, and publish on its
5 website a statewide disability indicator form which a person may complete and submit to the
6 department to notify primary and regional PSAP of certain disabilities. The disability indicator
7 form shall be available to subscribers of a wireless carrier, a wireline carrier or a prepaid wireless
8 telephone service. The disability indicator form shall be written in clear language that is
9 comprehensible to a person proficient in English at the fifth grade level and accessible to persons
10 with visual disabilities. In any place, where 5 per cent of citizens of the commonwealth speak a
1 of 2
11 language other than English as determined by the most recent American Community Survey of
12 the United States Census Bureau, the disability indicator form shall be translated into that
13 language.
14 The disability indicator form shall include space for the person to identify any mental
15 health needs, intellectual and developmental disabilities, complex medical needs or neurological
16 impairments, including, but not limited to: (i) the person has equipment required to sustain the
17 person’s life; (ii) the person is bedridden, a wheelchair user or has another mobility impairment;
18 (iii) the person is legally blind; (iv) the person is deaf or hard of hearing; (v) communication via
19 the phone may be by TTY; (vi) the person has a speech impairment; (vii) the person is
20 cognitively impaired; (viii) the person has a service animal; (ix) the person requires translation
21 services; or (x) any potential triggers for first responders to be aware of. The disability indicator
22 form shall also include a space for a person to remove or change any disability indicators.
23 (b) The department, in consultation with organizations representing the mobility, hearing,
24 speech and sight impaired communities, shall annually review the contents of the disability
25 indicator form and amend the disability indicator form when needed, as determined by the
26 department.
