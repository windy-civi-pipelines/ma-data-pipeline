HOUSE DOCKET, NO. 1186 FILED ON: 1/14/2025
HOUSE . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . No. 2640
By Representative Howitt of Seekonk, a petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 2640) of
Steven S. Howitt and David F. DeCoste relative to firearms furnished through the civilian
marksmanship program. Public Safety and Homeland Security.
[SIMILAR MATTER FILED IN PREVIOUS SESSION
SEE HOUSE, NO. 2345 OF 2023-2024.]
The Commonwealth of Massachusetts
_______________
In the One Hundred and Ninety-Fourth General Court
(2025-2026)
_______________
An Act relative to the Civilian Marksmanship Program.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court assembled, and by the authority
of the same, as follows:
1 Section 123 of Chapter 140 of the Massachusetts General Laws, as appearing in the 2022
2 Official Edition, is hereby amended by inserting a new paragraph:
3 "Notwithstanding any general law, special law, or regulation to the contrary, firearms
4 furnished through the Civilian Marksmanship Program as established by the United States
5 Congress shall be eligible for transfer by a licensee."
