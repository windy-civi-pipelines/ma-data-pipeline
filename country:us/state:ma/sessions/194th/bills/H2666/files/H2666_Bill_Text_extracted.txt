HOUSE DOCKET, NO. 2642 FILED ON: 1/16/2025
HOUSE . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . No. 2666
By Representatives McMurtry of Dedham and DeCoste of Norwell, a petition (accompanied by
bill, House, No. 2666) of Paul McMurtry and Jennifer Balinsky Armini relative to the Ancient
and Honorable Artillery Company of Massachusetts. Public Safety and Homeland Security.
[SIMILAR MATTER FILED IN PREVIOUS SESSION
SEE HOUSE DOCKET, NO. 4097 OF 2023-2024.]
The Commonwealth of Massachusetts
_______________
In the One Hundred and Ninety-Fourth General Court
(2025-2026)
_______________
An Act relative to preserving the ancient privileges of the Ancient and Honorable Artillery
Company of Massachusetts.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court assembled, and by the authority
of the same, as follows:
1 SECTION 1. Chapter 33 of the General Laws is hereby amended by striking out section
2 4, as appearing in the 2022 Official Edition, and inserting in place thereof the following section:
3 Section 4. Organization, composition. The active or organized militia shall be composed
4 of volunteers, and shall comprise the aides-de-camp of the commander-in-chief, the state staff,
5 the armed forces of the commonwealth, as defined in section 10, the National Lancers, the 54th
6 Massachusetts Volunteer Regiment, the Ancient and Honorable Artillery Company of
7 Massachusetts, and the retired list. The organized militia shall constitute the military division of
8 the executive branch of the Commonwealth.
1 of 2
9 SECTION 2. Chapter 33 of the General Laws is hereby amended by inserting the
10 following section:
11 Section 4C. Ancient and Honorable Artillery Company of Massachusetts. The Ancient
12 and Honorable Artillery Company of Massachusetts shall retain its ancient privileges as an
13 organized military company, as prescribed by the act of congress known as the National Defense
14 Act, subject, nevertheless, to all duties required by the law of militia. The Ancient and Honorable
15 Artillery Company shall be organized as the commander-in-chief directs and retain its name and
16 the right to wear distinctive uniforms as may be approved from time to time by the commander-
17 in-chief. It shall retain its constitution and by-laws, including its methods of selecting its officers
18 and conducting its internal affairs, in so far as these are consistent with the laws of the
19 commonwealth and the laws of the United States, and shall serve as a ceremonial honor guard
20 for the commander-in-chief at state functions and otherwise as directed by the commander-in-
21 chief.
22 SECTION 3. Chapter 33 of the General Laws is hereby amended by striking out section
23 132, as appearing in the 2022 Official Edition.
2 of 2
