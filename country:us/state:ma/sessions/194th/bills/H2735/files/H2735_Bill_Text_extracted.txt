HOUSE DOCKET, NO. 733 FILED ON: 1/13/2025
HOUSE . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . No. 2735
By Representatives Xiarhos of Barnstable and Howitt of Seekonk, a petition (accompanied by
bill, House, No. 2735) of Steven George Xiarhos, Alyson M. Sullivan-Almeida and others
relative to establishing a fund for the care of retired police dogs. Public Safety and Homeland
Security.
[SIMILAR MATTER FILED IN PREVIOUS SESSION
SEE HOUSE, NO. 2424 OF 2023-2024.]
The Commonwealth of Massachusetts
_______________
In the One Hundred and Ninety-Fourth General Court
(2025-2026)
_______________
An Act establishing a fund for the care of retired police dogs.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court assembled, and by the authority
of the same, as follows:
1 Chapter 29 of the General Laws, as appearing in the 2020 Official Edition, is hereby
2 amended by inserting after section 2QQQQQ thereof the following new section:-
3 Section 2RRRRR. (a) There shall be a Retired Police Dog Care Fund that shall be
4 administered by the secretary of the executive office of public safety and security. The fund shall
5 consist of amounts credited to the fund from: (i) any appropriations, grants, gifts or other money
6 authorized by the general court or other parties and specifically designated to be credited to the
7 fund; and (ii) any income derived from the investment of amounts credited to the fund. Any
8 unexpended balance in the fund at the end of a fiscal year shall not revert to the General Fund
9 and shall be available for expenditure in the subsequent fiscal year.
1 of 2
10 (b) All amounts credited to the fund shall be used without further appropriation to make
11 grants to non-profit institutions located within the commonwealth for the purpose of providing
12 care and medical services to retired police dogs. The secretary of the executive office of public
13 safety and security shall be responsible for ensuring that funds are properly allocated to and used
14 by reputable organizations for the care and medical needs of retired police dogs. For purposes of
15 this section, a "police dog" shall be defined in section 1 of chapter 111C.
16 (c) Annually, not later than March 1, the secretary of the executive office of public safety
17 and security shall issue a report to the clerks of the senate and house of representatives, the joint
18 committee on public safety and homeland security, and the senate and house committees on
19 ways and means on the fund's activities including, but not limited to, amounts credited to the
20 fund, amounts expended from the fund and any unexpended balance.
2 of 2
