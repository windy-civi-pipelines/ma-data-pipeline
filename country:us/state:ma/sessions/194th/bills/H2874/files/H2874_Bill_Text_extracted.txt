HOUSE DOCKET, NO. 1401 FILED ON: 1/14/2025
HOUSE . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . No. 2874
By Representative Jones of North Reading, a petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 2874) of
Bradley H. Jones, Jr., and others relative to the Group Insurance Commission and Medicare
health benefit supplement plans. Public Service.
[SIMILAR MATTER FILED IN PREVIOUS SESSION
SEE HOUSE, NO. 2563 OF 2023-2024.]
The Commonwealth of Massachusetts
_______________
In the One Hundred and Ninety-Fourth General Court
(2025-2026)
_______________
An Act relative to the Group Insurance Commission and Medicare migration.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court assembled, and by the authority
of the same, as follows:
1 SECTION 1. Section 18 of chapter 32A of the General Laws is amended by inserting at
2 the end thereof the following sentences:-
3 Notwithstanding any other provision of this chapter, all retirees, their spouses and
4 dependents, who are insured or eligible to be insured under sections five, ten B and twelve, age
5 65 or over and not eligible for coverage under medicare part A at no cost to the retiree, spouse,
6 or dependent, shall be transferred by the commission to medicare part A and part B; provided,
7 that the commission shall pay the medicare part A premium and any medicare part A or part B
8 premium penalty assessed by the federal government on said retirees, spouses and dependents as
9 result of transfer into medicare. Each retiree, to be transferred, shall: (a) provide the commission,
10 in such form as the commission shall prescribe, such information as is necessary to transfer to
1 of 2
11 medicare Part A and Part B, and (b) enroll in a medicare health benefits supplement plan offered
12 by the commission under section ten C or section fourteen; provided, that benefits under said
13 plan and medicare part A and part B together shall be of comparable actuarial value to those
14 under the retiree's existing coverage.
15 SECTION 2. The commission shall transfer retirees, their spouses and dependents, in
16 accordance with section 1 of this act, on or before July 1, 2026.
2 of 2
