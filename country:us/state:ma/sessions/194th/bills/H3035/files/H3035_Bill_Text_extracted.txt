HOUSE DOCKET, NO. 474 FILED ON: 1/9/2025
HOUSE . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . No. 3035
By Representative Cabral of New Bedford, a petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 3035) of
Antonio F. D. Cabral relative to the taxation of graduate student loan debt. Revenue.
[SIMILAR MATTER FILED IN PREVIOUS SESSION
SEE HOUSE, NO. 2722 OF 2023-2024.]
The Commonwealth of Massachusetts
_______________
In the One Hundred and Ninety-Fourth General Court
(2025-2026)
_______________
An Act to reduce graduate student loan debt.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court assembled, and by the authority
of the same, as follows:
1 SECTION 1. Paragraph (12) of subsection (a) of Part B of section 3 of chapter 62 of the
2 General Laws, as appearing in the 2022 Official Edition, is hereby amended by:
3 (a) striking out, in line 92, the word “or” the first place it appears.
4 (b) inserting after the word “two-year”, in line 92, a comma; and
5 (c) inserting after the word “college”, in line 92, the following words: - “, or graduate
6 education debt”.
7 SECTION 2. Paragraph (12) of said subsection (a) of said Part B of said section 3 of
8 said chapter 62, as so appearing, is hereby further amended by inserting after the second sentence
9 the following sentence: -
1 of 2
10 “For the purposes of this subparagraph, the term ‘graduate education debt’ shall mean
11 any loan which was or is administered by the financial aid office of a non-profit institution
12 offering a graduate or professional degree program at which the taxpayer, or a dependent of such
13 taxpayer, pursuant to subparagraph (3) of paragraph (b) of part B of this section, was enrolled as
14 a graduate or professional degree student and which loan has been secured through a state
15 student loan program, a federal student loan program or a commercial lender and which loan was
16 obtained and expended solely for the purposes of paying tuition and other expenses directly
17 related to such graduate or professional student enrollment.”.
