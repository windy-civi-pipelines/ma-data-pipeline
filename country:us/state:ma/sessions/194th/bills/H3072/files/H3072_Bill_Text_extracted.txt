HOUSE DOCKET, NO. 4014 FILED ON: 1/17/2025
HOUSE . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . No. 3072
By Representative Day of Stoneham (by request), a petition (accompanied by bill, House, No.
3072) of Vincent Lawrence Dixon relative to providing certain individuals with a vaccination tax
credit. Revenue.
The Commonwealth of Massachusetts
_______________
In the One Hundred and Ninety-Fourth General Court
(2025-2026)
_______________
An Act for an enabling authority - Vaccination Tax Credit.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court assembled, and by the authority
of the same, as follows:
1 SECTION 1. The Massachusetts General Laws are hereby amended by inserting the
2 following new chapter:
3 Chapter of Massachusetts General Laws – Enabling Authority – Vaccination Tax Credit
4 Section 1. An Act to create an Enabling Authority – Vaccination Tax Credit (VTC),
5 under the Laws of Massachusetts, providing for a Tax Credit, to certain qualified individuals, for
6 specific assistance in encouraging vaccination.
7 Section 2. It is in the interest of Public Health, and Public Safety, that as many residents
8 of Massachusetts, be vaccinated, as much, and as soon as it is possible, based on availability of
9 vaccine, and logistics to administer such vaccine; and vaccines relating to significant viruses, and
10 diseases.
1 of 2
11 Section 3. It being understandable, that many individuals, have realistic concern, about
12 the safety of vaccines, and should be encouraged to have the vaccines administered, as long as
13 the vaccines have been determined by scientific authorities, to be safe.
14 It is in the economic interests of all persons in Massachusetts, that wide, and appropriate
15 vaccination campaigns, can lead to a high percentage of vaccination of the population, that our
16 Commonwealth, may be freed, and/or reduced of the tyranny of significant viruses, and diseases,
17 in particular; so as to improve the overall health of our people, and the strength of our economy.
18 Therefore, this legislation authorizes an Enabling Authority – Vaccination Tax Credit
19 (VTC), of at least $100, to each individual, who attaches to their Massachusetts Individual
20 Income Tax, the Medical Documentation, of having received the available, and required doses,
21 of approved vaccines, approved by the Massachusetts Department of Public Health.
2 of 2
