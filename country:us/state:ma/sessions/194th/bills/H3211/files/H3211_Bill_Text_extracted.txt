HOUSE . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . No. 3211
By Representative Philips of Sharon, a petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 3211) of
Edward R. Philips and Dennis C. Gallagher relative to deeds excise receipts. Revenue.
[SIMILAR MATTER FILED IN PREVIOUS SESSION
SEE HOUSE, NO. 2922 OF 2023-2024.]
The Commonwealth of Massachusetts
_______________
In the One Hundred and Ninety-Fourth General Court
(2025-2026)
_______________
An Act relative to deeds excise receipts.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court assembled, and by the authority
of the same, as follows:
1 SECTION 1. Chapter 64D of the General Laws is hereby amended by inserting after
2 Section 1 the following section:-
3 Section 1A. (a) Notwithstanding any other general or special law to the contrary, the
4 counties of Bristol, Dukes, Nantucket, Norfolk and Plymouth shall retain 20 per cent of all deeds
5 excise receipts received on or after July 1, 2025, in addition to the amounts of deeds excise
6 receipts retained by said counties pursuant to any other statute or regulation. Such funds shall be
7 deposited to the General Fund of the county, and shall be disbursed and expended for meeting
8 the costs of the operation and maintenance of the county, or retained by the county for such
9 future use.
1 of 2
10 (b) Notwithstanding any other general or special law to the contrary, the county of
11 Barnstable shall retain 7.5 per cent of all deeds excise receipts received pursuant to this chapter
12 on or after July 1, 2025, in addition to the amounts of deeds excise receipts retained by said
13 county pursuant to any other statute or regulation. Such funds shall be deposited to the General
14 Fund of the county, and shall be disbursed and expended for meeting the costs of the operation
15 and maintenance of the county, or retained by the county for such future use.
16 SECTION 2. This act shall take effect upon its passage.
2 of 2
