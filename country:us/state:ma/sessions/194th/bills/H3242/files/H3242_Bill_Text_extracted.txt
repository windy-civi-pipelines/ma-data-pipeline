By Representative Smola of Warren, a petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 3242) of Todd
M. Smola and Donald R. Berthiaume, Jr., for legislation to establish a property tax exemption for
members of the National Guard. Revenue.
[SIMILAR MATTER FILED IN PREVIOUS SESSION
SEE HOUSE, NO. 2951 OF 2023-2024.]
The Commonwealth of Massachusetts
_______________
In the One Hundred and Ninety-Fourth General Court
(2025-2026)
_______________
An Act establishing a property tax exemption for members of the National Guard.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court assembled, and by the authority
of the same, as follows:
1 Section 5 of chapter 59 of the General Laws is hereby amended by inserting after clause
2 Twenty-second D, inserted by chapter 260 of the acts of 2022, the following clause:-
3 Twenty-second I, Real estate to the full amount of the taxable valuation of real property
4 of members of the National Guard or National Guard reservists who were domiciled in the
5 commonwealth for at least 6 months before entering service or who were domiciled in the
6 commonwealth for 5 consecutive years next before the date for filing for exemption under this
7 clause, which is occupied by them or their spouse as their domicile for any period of active
8 National Guard service outside the commonwealth plus 180 days; provided, however, that if
9 more than 1 period of active duty outside the commonwealth is performed during the year, the
10 additional 180 days shall be added to only 1 such period of active duty.
1 of 2
11 No real estate shall be so exempt which has been conveyed to evade taxation. Three-
12 quarters of the amount of the exemption shall be borne by the commonwealth, and the state
13 treasurer shall annually reimburse the city or town for such portion of the tax which otherwise
14 would have been collected for this exemption.
2 of 2
