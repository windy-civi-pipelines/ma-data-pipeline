HOUSE DOCKET, NO. 3193 FILED ON: 1/16/2025
HOUSE . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . No. 3278
By Representative Worrell of Boston, a petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 3278) of
Christopher J. Worrell for legislation to establish a graduated deed excise tax for affordable
housing. Revenue.
The Commonwealth of Massachusetts
_______________
In the One Hundred and Ninety-Fourth General Court
(2025-2026)
_______________
An Act creating a graduated deed excise tax for affordable housing.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court assembled, and by the authority
of the same, as follows:
1 SECTION 1. Section 1 of chapter 64D of the General Laws, as appearing in the 2022
2 Official Edition, is hereby amended by adding the following after the words “after two dollars”
3 as it appears the second time and before the word “provided”:-
4 “hereafter referred to as the existing rate”.
5 And in said section, after the words “two dollars” and before the word
6 “Notwithstanding” the following words:-
7 however, for sales exceeding $250,000 but less than $500,000, 1.1 multiplied by the
8 existing rate; for sales exceeding $500,000 but less than $1,000,000, 1.6 multiplied by the
9 existing rate, for sales exceeding $1,000,000 but less than $2,000,000 1.8 multiplied by the
10 existing rate; for sales exceeding $2,000,000 the existing rate multiplied by 2.
1 of 2
11 SECTION 2. Said section 1 of chapter 64D is hereby further amended by inserting after
12 the words "or the United States or any of their agencies are a party." the following words:-
13 "The Executive Office of Housing and Livable Communities shall equally divide such
14 tax revenue to initiatives to address the housing crisis and to fund the Affordable Housing Act
15 initiatives as established in chapter 150 of the Acts of 2024."
2 of 2
