HOUSE DOCKET, NO. 2801 FILED ON: 1/16/2025
HOUSE . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . No. 3558
By Representative Roy of Franklin, a petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 3558) of Jeffrey
N. Roy relative to electric vehicle charging stations. Telecommunications, Utilities and Energy.
The Commonwealth of Massachusetts
_______________
In the One Hundred and Ninety-Fourth General Court
(2025-2026)
_______________
An Act relative to electric vehicle charging stations.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court assembled, and by the authority
of the same, as follows:
1 SECTION 1. Chapter 93E of the General Laws is hereby amended by adding the
2 following section:
3 Section 10. Gasoline station; dealer; electric vehicle charging station required.
4 1. Definitions. As used in this section:
5 (a) "gasoline station" shall mean a premises or facility greater than fifteen hundred square
6 feet with operational gas dispensing motor fuel pumps that dispense motor fuel available for
7 general public retail sale.
8 (b) "dealer" shall mean any person owning or operating a premises or facility with one or
9 more operational gas dispensing motor fuel pumps that dispense motor fuel available for general
10 public retail sale.
1 of 2
11 (c) "electric vehicle charging station" shall mean a level three fast charging station that
12 delivers electricity from a source outside an electric vehicle. An electric vehicle charging station
13 may include several charge points simultaneously connecting several electric vehicles to the
14 station and any related equipment needed to facilitate charging plug-in electric vehicles. Each
15 electric vehicle charging station shall have non-proprietary chargers capable of charging electric
16 vehicles from at least three different incorporated auto manufacturers.
17 2. Any dealer installing new operational gas dispensing motor fuel pumps for the retail
18 sale of motor fuels for use in motor vehicles shall also assess its proximity to optimal sites along
19 or near commonwealth highways and major roadways for the hosting of electric vehicle fast
20 charging hubs and fleet depots, as identified by the electric vehicle coordinating council in its
21 most recent assessment, as required pursuant to subsection (d) of section 103 of chapter 239 of
22 the acts of 2024. Any dealer installing new operational gas dispensing motor fuel pumps in said
23 optimal sites shall also install at least one electric vehicle charging station at such gasoline
24 station.
25 3. No building permit shall be issued to a dealer if the documentation submitted for such
26 building permit does not reflect the completion of a proximity assessment pursuant to subsection
27 2 and, if said proximity assessment identifies that such gasoline station is located in an optimal
28 site for the hosting of electric vehicle fast charging hubs and fleet depots, the documentation
29 submitted for such building permit does not reflect the installation of at least one electric vehicle
30 charging station at such gasoline station.
