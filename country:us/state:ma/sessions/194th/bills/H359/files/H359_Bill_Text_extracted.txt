HOUSE DOCKET, NO. 2122 FILED ON: 1/15/2025
HOUSE . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . No. 359
By Representative Decker of Cambridge, a petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 359) of
Marjorie C. Decker for legislation to assure coverage and reimbursement for covered health care
services and to establish a community health worker workforce development task force
(including members of the General Court) to provide health equity. Consumer Protection and
Professional Licensure.
The Commonwealth of Massachusetts
_______________
In the One Hundred and Ninety-Fourth General Court
(2025-2026)
_______________
An Act relative to health equity and community health workers.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court assembled, and by the authority
of the same, as follows:
1 SECTION 1. Section 259 of chapter 112 of the General Laws is hereby amended by
2 striking out the definition of “Core competencies” and inserting in place thereof the following:-
3 ''Core competencies'', a set of overlapping and mutually reinforcing skills and knowledge
4 essential for effective community health work in core areas that include, but are not limited to:
5 (a) outreach methods and strategies;
6 (b) client and community assessment;
7 (c) effective communication;
8 (d) culturally-based communication and care;
9 (e) health education for behavior change;
1 of 5
10 (f) support, advocacy and coordination of care for clients;
11 (g) navigation and connection to resources for health needs, including but not
12 limited to behavioral health, mental health and substance use disorder services;
13 (h) application of public health concepts and approaches;
14 (i) community capacity building; and
15 (j) writing and technical communication skills.
16 SECTION 2. Section 260 of said chapter 112 is hereby amended by striking out the third
17 paragraph in its entirety.
18 SECTION 3. (a) As used in this section, the following term shall have the following
19 meaning:
20 “Community health worker services,” services provided by a community health worker,
21 including but not limited to, health education, information, and outreach; health promotion and
22 coaching; navigation of health care and community-based services, resources and systems; care
23 coordination; socioemotional support; and self-advocacy skill-building.
24 (b) Notwithstanding any general or special law to the contrary, the group insurance
25 commission public employee plans under Chapter 32A; the division of medical assistance under
26 chapter 118E and its contracted health insurers, health plans, health maintenance organizations,
27 accountable care organizations, behavioral health management firms and third-party
28 administrators under contract to a managed care organization or primary care clinician plan;
29 insurance companies organized under Chapter 175; non-profit hospital service corporations
2 of 5
30 organized under Chapter 176A; medical service corporations organized under chapter 176B; and
31 health maintenance organizations organized under chapter 176G shall provide coverage and
32 reimbursement for community health worker services delivered by a certified community health
33 worker, as defined by Section 259 of Chapter 112, or a community health worker in the process
34 of obtaining certification, who is employed by a health care provider entity, community-based
35 organization, or municipality, including entities that provide only non-medical health-related
36 services and support.
37 (c) The executive office of health and human services shall obtain any federal
38 authorization necessary to implement this section and maximize federal financial participation
39 for the coverage and benefits provided under this section.
40 (d) The office of Medicaid, the division of insurance and the group insurance commission
41 shall promulgate regulations or other guidance necessary to effectuate this section, including
42 establishing provider organization enrollment pathways for non-health care entities.
43 SECTION 4. (a) There shall be a Community Health Worker Workforce Development
44 Taskforce. The taskforce shall be chaired by the president of the senate or their designee and the
45 speaker of the house of representatives or their designee, and shall consist of the following
46 members: the senate chair of the joint committee on health care financing, the house chair of the
47 joint committee on health care financing, the senate chair of the joint committee of public health,
48 the house chair of the joint committee on public health, the secretary of health and human
49 services or their designee, the secretary of labor and workforce development or their designee,
50 the assistant secretary for MassHealth or their designee, the commissioner of public health or
51 their designee, the commissioner of mental health or their designee, the commissioner of the
3 of 5
52 division of insurance or their designee, the executive director of the Health Policy Commission
53 or their designee, a representative of the Massachusetts Association of Community Health
54 Workers, a representative of the Massachusetts League of Community Health Centers, a
55 representative of Health Care For All, a representative of the Massachusetts Health and Hospital
56 Association, a representative of the Association for Behavioral Healthcare, a representative of
57 the Massachusetts Public Health Alliance, a representative of a municipality employing
58 community health workers, a representative of a provider organization employing community
59 health workers, and a representative of a community-based organization employing community
60 health workers.
61 (b) The taskforce shall study and assess: (i) the existing community health worker
62 workforce, including but not limited to: the current supply and distribution of community health
63 workers by demographics, including age, race, ethnicity, language, disability status, sexual
64 orientation, gender identity and other characteristics currently available through existing data
65 sources; certification status; city, municipality or region of the state; role and scope of services
66 provided; and employer type; (ii) the state of certification of community health workers,
67 including barriers to certification; (iii) the sustainability of reimbursement of community health
68 workers in health care and community-based settings; and (iv) opportunities and challenges in
69 community health worker recruitment, retention, and career pathway development, including
70 advancement in the health care workforce.
71 (c) The taskforce shall issue a report with the findings under subsection (b) and
72 recommendations on: (i) making data on community health workers collected by state agencies
73 and contracted entities publicly available and actionable; (ii) addressing barriers to community
74 health worker certification; (iii) standardizing community health worker roles based on core
4 of 5
75 competencies; (iv) increasing community health worker recruitment, retention and career
76 pathways for clinical and non-clinical roles; (v) building capacity among employers to
77 effectively support and supervise community health workers; and (vi) creating a sustainable
78 reimbursement structure for community health worker services, including considerations for
79 providing a living wage.
80 (d) The taskforce shall file its report, including any recommendations for proposed
81 legislation or regulations, to the clerks of the house of representatives and senate, the joint
82 committee on health care financing and the joint committee on public health no later than March
83 31, 2026.
