By Representative Murray of Milford, a petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 3747) of
Brian W. Murray relative to duplicate motor vehicle license plates. Transportation.
[SIMILAR MATTER FILED IN PREVIOUS SESSION
SEE HOUSE, NO. 3433 OF 2023-2024.]
The Commonwealth of Massachusetts
_______________
In the One Hundred and Ninety-Fourth General Court
(2025-2026)
_______________
An Act relative to duplicate license plates.
Whereas, The deferred operation of this act would tend to defeat its purpose, which is to
regulate the issuance of duplicate license plates, therefore it is hereby declared to be an
emergency law, necessary for the immediate preservation of the public safety.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court assembled, and by the authority
of the same, as follows:
1 SECTION 1. Section 2 of chapter 90 of the General Laws, as appearing in the 2018
2 Official Edition, is hereby amended in line 156 by inserting after the word “provided,” the
3 following:-
4 “that the registrar shall not furnish a number plate with the identical sequence of
5 horizontally aligned alphanumeric characters as another active number plate; and provided
6 further,”.
7 SECTION 2. The registrar shall issue a new registration number and number plates to a
8 registrant, at the registrant’s option upon application, without charge, whose number plate shares
1 of 2
9 the identical sequence of horizontally aligned alphanumeric characters with another active
10 number plate.
11 SECTION 3. Not later than January 1, 2025, the registrar shall (i) identify all registrants
12 with number plates that share the identical sequence of horizontally aligned alphanumeric
13 characters with another active number plate and (ii) provide written notice to such registrants
14 informing them of same and of the registrar’s obligations under section 2.
15 SECTION 4. Municipal, county, and state agencies and other governmental entities,
16 including but not limited to law enforcement agencies and personnel, the Massachusetts
17 department of transportation, and Massachusetts Bay Transportation Authority, and those acting
18 on their behalf, shall include all vertically stacked characters that are part of a registration
19 number and shall note whether a plate is a specialty, distinctive, commercial or other plate series
20 when documenting or otherwise recording a motor vehicle or trailer plate number for the purpose
21 of assessing any violation, fine, surcharge, toll, or fee.
