By Representative Ramos of Springfield, a petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 3767) of
Orlando Ramos relative to removing the driving restrictions on a car with antique tags.
Transportation.
[SIMILAR MATTER FILED IN PREVIOUS SESSION
SEE HOUSE, NO. 3412 OF 2023-2024.]
The Commonwealth of Massachusetts
_______________
In the One Hundred and Ninety-Fourth General Court
(2025-2026)
_______________
An Act relative to removing the driving restrictions on a car with antique tags.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court assembled, and by the authority
of the same, as follows:
1 SECTION 1. Section 1 of chapter 90 of the General Laws, as appearing in the 2020
2 Official Edition, is hereby amended by striking out the definition “Antique motor car” and
3 inserting in place thereof the following definition:-
4 “Antique motor car”, any motor vehicle that is over 25 years old and not used by the
5 owner primarily for general transportation purposes. The application for registration shall be
6 accompanied by an affidavit upon a form provided by the registrar, which shall include:
7 (i) a statement by the owner that the antique motor vehicle is owned and operated solely
8 as an antique item and not primarily for general transportation purposes; and
1 of 2
9 (ii) registration of another motor vehicle, in the owner’s name that is currently used by
10 the owner for daily transportation purposes.
11 SECTION 2. Section 33 of said chapter 90, as so appearing, is hereby amended by
12 striking out the (13) paragraph and inserting in place thereof the following paragraph:- For the
13 registration of every antique motor car; provided that said fee shall be collected every 2 years.
