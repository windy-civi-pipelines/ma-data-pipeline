HOUSE DOCKET, NO. 2813 FILED ON: 1/16/2025
HOUSE . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . No. 3948
By Representative Sabadosa of Northampton, a petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 3948)
of Lindsay N. Sabadosa for legislation to establish rights and career advancement for contingent
faculty in public higher education. Higher Education.
The Commonwealth of Massachusetts
_______________
In the One Hundred and Ninety-Fourth General Court
(2025-2026)
_______________
An Act to establish comprehensive rights and career advancement for contingent faculty in
public higher education.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court assembled, and by the authority
of the same, as follows:
1 SECTION 1. Chapter 15A of the General Laws is hereby amended by striking out section
2 9 and inserting in place thereof the following section:-
3 Section 9. The board shall establish employment standards for contingent faculty
4 members, which shall include:
5 (i) the following minimum compensation requirements: (A) base per-course rates no less
6 than the pro-rated equivalent of full-time faculty compensation; (B) equal pay rates between day
7 division and continuing education division; (C) regular cost-of-living adjustments; (D) additional
8 compensation for departmental service and committees; and (E) payment for course preparation
9 when classes are canceled within 30 days of start date;
10 (ii) the following employment security provisions: (A) standard 2-year contracts after 4
11 successful teaching semesters; (B) course assignment notification at least 45 days before term
1 of 6
12 start; (C) compensation of 25 per cent of course rate for late cancellations; (D) protection from
13 arbitrary non-renewal; and (E) right of first refusal for previously taught courses; and
14 (iii) professional support requirements including: (A) dedicated office space; (B)
15 institution-provided computer equipment; (C) access to departmental resources; (D) professional
16 development funding; (E) research and conference support; and (F) library and technology
17 access.
18 SECTION 2. Said chapter 15A is hereby further amended by inserting after section 9B
19 the following 2 sections:-
20 Section 9C. Each institution shall:
21 (i) establish a career advancement tier framework that:
22 (A) creates at least 3 tiers of contingent faculty positions with increasing job security,
23 compensation and benefits; (B) provides clear advancement criteria; (C) includes teaching
24 excellence and institutional service; and (D) ensures fairness and transparency;
25 (ii) provide internal advancement opportunities including: (A) priority consideration for
26 full-time positions; (B) credit for years of service; (C) recognition of institutional knowledge;
27 and (D) support for professional development; and
28 (iii) ensure departmental integration through:
29 (A) participation in governance; (B) voting rights on departmental matters; (C) inclusion
30 in faculty meetings; and (D) access to grants and research support.
2 of 6
31 Section 9D. All institutions shall: (i) eliminate pay disparities between day and
32 continuing education divisions within 2 years; (ii) place all new contingent faculty hires in day
33 division; (iii) provide equal benefits and advancement opportunities regardless of division; and
34 (iv) maintain consistent evaluation and advancement procedures.
35 SECTION 3. Section 10 of said chapter 15A, as appearing in the 2022 Official Edition, is
36 hereby amended by inserting before the definition of "Community college" the following
37 definition:-
38 "Career advancement tier", a classification level established by a public institution of
39 higher education for contingent faculty members that establishes specific rights, responsibilities
40 and compensation rates based on teaching experience and institutional service.
41 SECTION 4. Said section 10 of said chapter 15A, as so appearing, is hereby further
42 amended by inserting after the definition of "Community college affiliate" the following 4
43 definitions:-
44 "Contingent faculty member", an instructor at a public institution of higher education
45 who: (i) teaches at least 1 course per academic year; (ii) is compensated on a per-course basis;
46 and (iii) is not in a tenure-track position.
47 "Day division", credit-bearing courses offered as part of regular academic programs
48 during standard operational hours.
49 "Continuing education division", credit and non-credit courses offered through
50 continuing education, graduate programs, evening or extended learning programs.
3 of 6
51 "Full-time equivalent load", the standard course load assigned to full-time faculty
52 members at the institution.
53 SECTION 5. Said chapter 15A hereby further amended by adding the following 4
54 sections:-
55 Section 50. (a) Contingent faculty members shall be eligible for group insurance
56 commission benefits, pursuant to chapter 32A, if they: (i) teach courses equivalent to 25 per cent
57 or more of a full-time load; or (ii) have taught at least 2 courses per year for the previous 2 years.
58 (b) Eligible faculty shall receive: (i) pro-rated premium contributions based on teaching
59 load; (ii) access to all standard plan options; and
60 (iii) continuation of coverage between semesters if maintaining a regular teaching
61 schedule.
62 Section 51. Public institutions of higher education shall, to the extent possible:
63 (i) provide institutional matching for mandatory employee OBRA 1990 retirement
64 contributions or establish mechanisms for contingent faculty participation in Social Security;
65 (ii) maintain 457b eligibility;
66 (iii) provide retirement planning support; and
67 (iv) include part-time faculty representation in benefit decisions.
68 Section 52. (a) Institutions shall maintain records including: (i) courses taught and
69 compensation; (ii) career advancement tier status; (iii) benefits eligibility; (iv) professional
70 development participation; and (v) evaluation results.
4 of 6
71 (b) Faculty members shall receive written notice of: (i) course assignments and
72 compensation;(ii) benefits eligibility and options; (iii) advancement opportunities; and (iv)
73 evaluation criteria and results.
74 Section 53. (a) Institutions shall provide: (i) annual professional development funding;
75 (ii) access to research grants; (iii) conference attendance support; (iv) technology and resource
76 access; and (v) mentoring opportunities.
77 SECTION 6. Chapter 29 of the General Laws is hereby amended by inserting after
78 section 2JJJJJJ the following section:-
79 Section 2KKKKKK. (a) There shall be established and set up on the books of the
80 commonwealth a separate fund knows as the Contingent Faculty Career Advancement Fund. The
81 fund shall receive:
82 (i) institutional contributions;
83 (ii) appropriations or other money authorized or transferred by the general court and
84 specifically designated to be credited to the fund;
85 (iii) funds from public and private sources, including, but not limited to, gifts, grants and
86 donations; and (iv) any interest earned on the assets of the fund.
87 (b) Amounts credited to the fund may be expended without further appropriation by the
88 board of higher education to support: (i) the implementation of career frameworks established in
89 chapter 15A; (ii) professional development programs for faculty members of public institutions
90 of higher education; (iii) position conversion of contingent faculty members, as defined in
91 section 10 of said chapter 15A, to full-time status; (iv) benefits expansion for faculty members of
5 of 6
92 public institutions of higher education; and (v) resource provision to faculty members of public
93 institutions of higher education.
94 SECTION 7. (a) The board of higher education shall: (i) promulgate detailed regulations,
95 pursuant to chapter 30A, to implement this act; (ii) monitor institutional compliance; (iii)
96 investigate violations; (iv) issue corrective orders; (v) impose penalties for non-compliance; and
97 (vi) establish an advisory committee with both student and contingent faculty representation to
98 advise the oversight and implementation of the contingent faculty career advancement fund.
99 (b) Faculty members may: (i) file complaints with the board; (ii) appeal adverse
100 decisions; (iii) seek enforcement through civil action; and (iv) receive protection from retaliation.
101 SECTION 8. The board of higher education shall promulgate regulations to implement
102 this act no later than January 1, 2026.
103 SECTION 9. Sections 1 through 7 shall take effect on July 1, 2026.
