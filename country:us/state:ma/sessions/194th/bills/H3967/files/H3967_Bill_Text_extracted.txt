HOUSE DOCKET, NO. 1959 FILED ON: 1/15/2025
HOUSE . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . No. 3967
By Representative Flanagan of Dennis, a petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 3967) of
Christopher Richard Flanagan for legislation to further regulate surcharges on real property.
Revenue.
The Commonwealth of Massachusetts
_______________
In the One Hundred and Ninety-Fourth General Court
(2025-2026)
_______________
An Act relative to amending the Community Preservation Act to include an extra percentage
surcharge for accessibility.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court assembled, and by the authority
of the same, as follows:
1 Chapter 44B of the General Laws is hereby amended by inserting after section 7 the
2 following section:-
3 Section 7A. (a) This section shall take effect in any city or town upon the approval by the
4 legislative body and its acceptance by the voters of a ballot question as set forth in section 3;
5 provided, that the ballot language shall be in the form provided in subsection (c); provided
6 further, that the city or town has accepted sections 3 to 7, inclusive.
7 (b) Notwithstanding the provisions of chapter 59 or any other general or special law to
8 the contrary, the legislative body may vote to accept this section by approving a surcharge on
9 real property of 1 per cent of the real estate tax levy against real property. The amount of the
10 surcharge shall not be included in a calculation of total taxes assessed for purposes of section
11 21C of said chapter 59.
1 of 2
12 (c) Upon approval by the legislative body, the actions of the body shall be submitted for
13 acceptance to the voters of a city or town at the next regular municipal or state election. The city
14 or town clerk or the state secretary shall place it on the ballot in the form of the following
15 question:
16 ''Shall this (city or town) accept section 7A of chapter 44B of the General Laws, as
17 approved by its legislative body, a summary of which appears below''
18 (Set forth here a fair, concise summary and purpose of the law to be acted upon, as
19 determined by the city solicitor or town counsel.)
20 (d) Upon acceptance of this section, and upon the assessors' warrant to the tax collector,
21 the accepted surcharge shall be imposed pursuant to section 4.
22 (e) A city or town that accepts this section shall add 1 additional member to the
23 community preservation committee established pursuant to section 5, who shall be a member of
24 the municipality’s disability commission or other municipal commission related to disability or
25 accessibility, or, if no such commission exists or no such member is available, who shall be
26 appointed by the select board or city council.
27 (f) In each fiscal year and upon the recommendation of the community preservation
28 committee, the legislative body shall spend, or set aside for later spending, not less than 10 per
29 cent of the annual revenues in its Community Preservation Fund for enhancing the physical
30 accessibility of public indoor and outdoor spaces in the community.
2 of 2
