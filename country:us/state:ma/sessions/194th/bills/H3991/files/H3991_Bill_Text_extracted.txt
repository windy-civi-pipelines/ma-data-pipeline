By Representative Diggs of Barnstable, a petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 3991) of Kip
A. Diggs for legislation to further regulate homesteads. The Judiciary.
The Commonwealth of Massachusetts
_______________
In the One Hundred and Ninety-Fourth General Court
(2025-2026)
_______________
An Act relative to ensuring the equitable protection of homestead.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court assembled, and by the authority
of the same, as follows:
1 SECTION 1. Section 2 of chapter 188 of the General Laws, as appearing in the 2022
2 Official Edition, is hereby amended by adding the following subsection:-
3 (e) Notwithstanding any provision of this chapter to the contrary, an owner of a home
4 who is at least 65 years old or an owner of a home who has a documented disability shall receive
5 an additional homestead exemption equal to 25 per cent of the baseline exemption for their
6 county. For purposes of this subsection, “documented disability” shall mean a medically-
7 determinable, permanent physical or mental impairment that meets the disability requirements
8 for Supplemental Security Income under 42 U.S.C. 1382c(a)(3)(A) and 42 U.S.C.
9 1382c(a)(3)(C) that is documented and in effect at the time of recording.
10 SECTION 2. Said chapter 188 is hereby further amended by adding following section:-
11 Section 15. (a) For purposes of this section, the word “department” shall mean the
12 department of revenue.
1 of 3
13 (b) The homestead exemption shall provide financial protection to homeowners by
14 safeguarding a portion of the equity in their principal residence from creditors’ claims, subject to
15 the following:
16 (1) The homestead exemption shall be calculated annually by the department based on a
17 percentage of the median home value for each county, as determined by statewide property tax
18 assessments. The exemption shall not be less than $250,000 nor exceed $1,250,000, ensuring
19 equitable protection across all counties in the commonwealth.
20 (2) For properties owned by joint tenants, tenants by the entirety or trust beneficiaries, the
21 homestead exemption amount shall be applied in full to the property, with no proration among
22 co-owners. If multiple declarations are filed for the same property, the maximum cumulative
23 exemption shall not exceed the market-adjusted limit pursuant to subsection (b) for the county.
24 (3) The department shall annually adjust the minimum and maximum homestead
25 exemption thresholds based on the consumer price index to account for changes in the cost of
26 living in the commonwealth.
27 (4) First-time homebuyers earning less than 120 per cent of the area median income may
28 qualify for a temporary 10 per cent increase in the homestead exemption during their first 5 years
29 of ownership.
30 (c) The secretary of the commonwealth shall develop and implement a statewide
31 education campaign to ensure homeowners are aware of their rights under this chapter,
32 including: (i) publishing multilingual materials on the homestead declaration process; (ii) hosting
33 informational sessions in partnership with municipalities and housing advocacy groups; and
2 of 3
34 (iii) streamlining the declaration filing process, including online submission options.
35 (d) The department shall prepare an annual report that shall include: (i) the number of
36 homestead declarations filed by county; (ii) the impact of market-adjusted exemptions on
37 homeowner protections; and (iii) any recommendations for further improvements to the
38 homestead exemption based on economic and demographic trends. The report shall be submitted,
39 no later than December 31 of each year, to the chairs of the joint committee on housing, the
40 chairs of the joint committee on revenue and the clerks of the house of representatives and
41 senate.
42 SECTION 3. This act shall take effect on January 1, 2026.
3 of 3
