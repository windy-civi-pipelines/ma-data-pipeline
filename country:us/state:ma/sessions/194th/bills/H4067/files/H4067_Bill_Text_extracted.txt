HOUSE DOCKET, NO. 2436 FILED ON: 1/16/2025
HOUSE . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . No. 4067
By Representative Diggs of Barnstable, a petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 4067) of Kip
A. Diggs relative to expanding and modernizing apprenticeships. Labor and Workforce
Development.
The Commonwealth of Massachusetts
_______________
In the One Hundred and Ninety-Fourth General Court
(2025-2026)
_______________
An Act relative to expanding and modernizing apprenticeships.
Whereas, The deferred operation of this act would tend to defeat its purpose, which is to
address expanding apprenticeship opportunities in the Commonwealth, therefore it is hereby
declared to be an emergency law, necessary for the immediate preservation of the public
convenience.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court assembled, and by the authority
of the same, as follows:
1 Chapter 23 of the General Laws is hereby amended by inserting after section 11W the
2 following section:-
3 Section 11X. (a) The division, in consultation with the apprenticeship council established
4 in section 11E, which for purposes of this section shall mean the green energy, advanced
5 manufacturing, hospitality and green building industries.
6 (b) The division shall expand apprenticeship opportunities in expansion industries by: (i)
7 developing industry-specific standards and training modules in partnership with employers, trade
8 organizations and educational institutions; (ii) establishing regional advisory boards to address
9 local workforce needs and ensure alignment with regional economic development priorities; and
1 of 3
10 (iii) prioritizing apprenticeship opportunities for underrepresented populations, including
11 women, minorities, veterans and individuals with disabilities.
12 (c) Employers participating in an apprentice program in expansion industries shall be
13 eligible for: (i) a tax credit of up to $5,000 per apprentice hired; provided that, the apprentice
14 completes at least 1,000 hours of training; and (ii) state subsidies to offset costs associated with
15 apprentice wages, training materials and administrative expenses.
16 (d) To qualify for incentives pursuant to subsection (c), employers shall: (i) have or
17 establish an apprentice program as defined in section 11H; and (ii) demonstrate a commitment to
18 diversity and inclusion in their hiring practices.
19 (e)(1) The division shall launch a statewide public awareness campaign to promote
20 apprenticeship opportunities. The public awareness campaign shall focus on: (i) the benefits of
21 apprenticeships as a debt-free pathway to high-paying careers; (ii) success stories from current
22 and former apprentices; and (iii) resources for employers and potential apprentices to participate
23 in the program.
24 (2) The public awareness campaign shall include: (i) multimedia advertisements on
25 television, radio and digital platforms; and (ii) partnerships with schools, community
26 organizations and workforce development boards to distribute materials and host informational
27 sessions.
28 (f) The division shall prepare an annual report on the program established in this section
29 that shall include: (i) the number of apprentices registered by industry and region; (ii) employer
30 participation and utilization of incentives; (iii) the effectiveness of the public awareness
31 campaign; and (iv) any recommendations for improvements to the program. The annual report
2 of 3
32 shall be submitted, not later than December 31, to the chairs of the joint committee on labor and
33 workforce development and the clerks of the house of representatives and senate.
3 of 3
