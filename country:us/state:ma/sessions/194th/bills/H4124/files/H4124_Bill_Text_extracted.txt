HOUSE . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . No. 4124
By Representative Lewis of Framingham, a petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 4124) of
Jack Patrick Lewis and Samantha Monta単o relative to role-playing games in correctional
facilities. Public Safety and Homeland Security.
The Commonwealth of Massachusetts
_______________
In the One Hundred and Ninety-Fourth General Court
(2025-2026)
_______________
An Act relative to role-playing games in correctional facilities.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court assembled, and by the authority
of the same, as follows:
1 SECTION 1. Chapter 127 of the General Laws is hereby amended by adding the
2 following section:-
3 Section 172. (a) Notwithstanding any general or special law or regulation to the contrary,
4 a correctional facility shall not prohibit an inmate from playing or participating in roll-playing
5 games, including but not limited to Dungeons & Dragons and similar games, during any
6 regularly-allotted period of leisure or recreation afforded to the inmate.
7 (b) A correctional facility may adopt written policies to implement this section.
8 SECTION 2. This act shall take effect 90 days after passage.
