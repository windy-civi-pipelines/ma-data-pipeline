HOUSE . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . No. 4282
The Commonwealth of Massachusetts
_______________
In the One Hundred and Ninety-Fourth General Court
(2025-2026)
_______________
An Act authorizing the town of Palmer to grant additional licenses for the sale of all alcoholic
beverages.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court assembled, and by the authority
of the same, as follows:
1 SECTION 1. (a) Notwithstanding section 17 of chapter 138 of the General Laws, the
2 licensing authority of the town of Palmer may grant 5 additional licenses for the sale of all
3 alcoholic beverages as follows: (i) 1 license for the sale of all alcoholic beverages to be drunk on
4 the premises pursuant to section 12 of said chapter 138 to Tables at the Farm located at 3092
5 Palmer St; 1 license for the sale of all alcoholic beverages to be drunk on the premises pursuant
6 to said section 12 of said chapter 138 to Don Juans Cantina Palmer LLC located at 1519 N. Main
7 St; and 1 license for the sale of all alcoholic beverages to be drunk on the premises pursuant to
8 said section 12 of said chapter 138 to a Ernestina’s LLC located at 2054 Bridge St.; and (ii) 1
9 license for the sale of all alcoholic beverages not to be drunk on the premises pursuant to section
10 15 of said chapter 138 to Luke’s Beer and Wine located at 1478 N. Main St; and 1 license for
11 the sale of all alcoholic beverages not to be drunk on the premises pursuant to section 15 of said
12 chapter 138 to Jane Alden Convenience Store located at 1469 N. Main St. The licenses granted
13 under this section shall be subject to all of the said chapter 138 except said section 17.
1 of 3
14 (b) A license granted under clause (i) of subsection (a) of this section shall only be
15 exercised in the dining room of a common victualler and other public rooms or areas considered
16 reasonable and appropriate by the licensing authority as certified in writing.
17 (c) The licensing authority shall not approve the transfer of a license granted pursuant to
18 this section to any other location, but it may grant the license to a new applicant at the same
19 location if the applicant files with the licensing authority a letter from the department of revenue
20 and a letter from the department of unemployment assistance indicating that the license is in
21 good standing with those departments and that all applicable taxes, fees and contributions have
22 been paid.
23 (d) If a licensee terminates or fails to renew a license granted under this section or any
24 such license is cancelled, revoked or no longer in use, it shall be returned physically, with all
25 legal rights, privileges and restrictions pertaining thereto, to the licensing authority and the
26 licensing authority may, within 3 years after such return, then grant the license to a new applicant
27 at the same location under the same conditions as specified in this section, otherwise such license
28 shall dissolve.
29 (e) A license granted under this section shall be issued within 1 year after the effective
30 date of this act; provided, however, that a license originally granted within that time period may
31 be granted to a new applicant under subsections (c) and (d) thereafter.
32 SECTION 2. Upon issuance of the license authorized in clause (ii) of subsection (a) of
33 section 1, the licensee shall return physically, with all of the legal rights, privileges and
34 restrictions pertaining thereto, to the licensing authority, its license for the sale of wine and malt
35 beverages not to be drunk on the premises under section 15 of chapter 138.
2 of 3
36 SECTION 3. This act shall take effect upon its passage.
3 of 3
