HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES, September 29, 2025.
petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 1148) of Rodney M. Elliott,
James B. Eldridge and others relative to the practice and insurance
coverage of acupuncture, reports recommending that the accompanying
bill (House, No. 4548) ought to pass.
For the committee,
JAMES M. MURPHY.
FILED ON: 9/19/2025
HOUSE . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . No. 4548
The Commonwealth of Massachusetts
_______________
In the One Hundred and Ninety-Fourth General Court
(2025-2026)
_______________
An Act relative to the practice of acupuncture.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court assembled, and by the authority
of the same, as follows:
1 SECTION 2. Chapter 111 of the General Laws is hereby amended by adding the
2 following section:-
3 Section 249. There shall be a commission on acupuncture and wellness within the
4 department. The commission shall consist of: the commissioner of public health or the
5 commissioner’s designee, who shall chair the commission; the commissioner of insurance or the
6 commissioner’s designee; the director of MassHealth or the director’s designee; the director of
7 the board of registration in medicine or the director’s designee; the senate chair of the joint
8 committee on public health; the house chair of the joint committee on public health; a member
9 representing a statewide organization of licensed acupuncturists; a member representing a
10 statewide organization of medical acupuncturists; a member representing the Massachusetts
11 Public Health Association; and 5 members to be selected by the Governor including: no fewer
12 than 2 acupuncturists, licensed and practicing in the state of Massachusetts; a member
13 representing one of the top five health insurance companies in Massachusetts according to
1 of 5
14 market share, a member representing a health care consumer organization, and a member
15 currently practicing as a licensed physician in Massachusetts.
16 The commission on acupuncture and wellness shall make an investigation and
17 comprehensive study of the potential for better integrated use of acupuncture services to expand
18 access, reduce health care costs, and provide improved quality of care to Massachusetts
19 residents. The commission shall:
20 (1) Consider strategies to evaluate and implement effective integration of acupuncture
21 services in health care delivery in Massachusetts with specific focus on interventions in pain
22 management, substance abuse treatment, and wellness promotion.
23 (2) Consider strategies to effectively integrate acupuncture treatment modalities into
24 alternative payment models, including, but not limited to, accountable care organizations,
25 workplace wellness programs and provider organizations established under chapter 224 of the
26 acts of 2012.
27 (3) Consider strategies regarding reimbursement of licensed acupuncturists via third party
28 payors or otherwise to facilitate a stable and sustainable integration of acupuncture services into
29 the broader system of health care delivery.
30 The commission on acupuncture and wellness shall submit to the secretary of health and
31 human services and the joint committee public health, six months after the effective date of this
32 act and annually thereafter, a report that includes findings from the commission’s review along
33 with recommendations and any suggested legislation to implement those recommendations.
2 of 5
34 SECTION 3. Said chapter 175 is hereby amended by inserting before section 47CCC the
35 following section:-
36 Section 47AAA. (a) Any blanket or general policy of insurance described in subdivision
37 (A), (C), or (D) of section one hundred and ten which is issued or subsequently renewed by
38 agreement between the insurer and the policyholder, within or without the commonwealth,
39 during the period within which this premium is effective, or any policy of accident or sickness
40 insurance as described in section one hundred and eight which provides hospital expense and
41 surgical expense insurance and which is delivered or issued for delivery or subsequently renewed
42 by agreement between the insurer and the policyholder in the commonwealth, during the period
43 within which this provision is effective, or any employers' health and welfare fund which
44 provides hospital expense and surgical expense benefits and which is issued or renewed to any
45 person or group of persons in the commonwealth, during the period within which this provision
46 is effective, shall provide benefits for acupuncture and oriental medicine based diagnosis and
47 treatment in the areas of pain management, post-traumatic stress disorder, substance abuse
48 treatment, and nausea.
49 SECTION 4. Chapter 175 is hereby further amended by inserting after section 205 the
50 following section:-
51 Section 205A. (a) The commissioner shall not approve a policy under section 205 that
52 does not provide benefits for acupuncture and oriental medicine based diagnosis and treatment in
53 the areas of pain management, post-traumatic stress disorder, substance abuse treatment, and
54 nausea.
3 of 5
55 SECTION 5. Chapter 176A of the General Laws is hereby amended by inserting after
56 section 8DDD the following section:-
57 Section 8EEE. (a) Any contract between a subscriber and the corporation under an
58 individual or group hospital service plan delivered, issued or renewed in the commonwealth shall
59 provide as benefits to all individual subscribers and members within the commonwealth and to
60 all group members having a principal place of employment within the commonwealth
61 acupuncture and oriental medicine based diagnosis and treatment in the areas of pain
62 management, post-traumatic stress disorder, substance abuse treatment, and nausea.
63 SECTION 6. Chapter 176B of the General Laws is hereby amended by inserting after
64 section 4DDD the following section:-
65 Section 4EEE. (a) Any subscription certificate under an individual or group medical
66 service agreement delivered, issued or renewed in the commonwealth shall provide benefits to all
67 individual subscribers and members within the commonwealth and to all group members having
68 a principal place of employment within the commonwealth for acupuncture and oriental
69 medicine based diagnosis and treatment in the areas of pain management, post-traumatic stress
70 disorder, substance abuse treatment, and nausea.
71 SECTION 7. Chapter 176G of the General Laws is hereby amended by inserting after
72 section 4VV the following section:-
73 Section 4WW. (a) Any group health maintenance contract shall provide coverage for
74 acupuncture and oriental medicine-based diagnosis and treatment in the areas of pain
75 management, post-traumatic stress disorder, substance abuse treatment, and nausea.
4 of 5
76 SECTION 1. Chapter 32A of the General Laws is hereby amended by inserting after
77 Section 17Z the following section:-
78 Section 17AA. The commission shall provide to an active or retired employee of the
79 commonwealth who is insured under the group insurance commission coverage for acupuncture
80 and oriental medicine-based diagnosis and treatment in the areas of pain management, post-
81 traumatic stress disorder, substance abuse treatment, and nausea.
82 SECTION 8. Notwithstanding any general or special law to the contrary, no third-party
83 payer of health care services shall differentiate reimbursement rates for acupuncture services by
84 provider type. Only licensed acupuncturists or medical doctors shall be reimbursed for
85 acupuncture services.
5 of 5
