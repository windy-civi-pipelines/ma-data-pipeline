HOUSE . . . . . . . . No. 4637
The Commonwealth of Massachusetts

HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES, October 23, 2025.
(accompanied by bill, House, No. 1688) of Michael J. Finn relative to
penalties for unauthorized use of unmanned aerial vehicles; and the
petition (accompanied by bill, House, No. 1978) of Lindsay N. Sabadosa
and Patricia A. Duffy relative to the responsible use of certain advanced
robotic technologies, reports recommending that the accompanying bill
(House, No. 4637) ought to be pass.
For the committee,
MICHAEL S. DAY.
FILED ON: 10/12/2025
HOUSE . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . No. 4637
The Commonwealth of Massachusetts
_______________
In the One Hundred and Ninety-Fourth General Court
(2025-2026)
_______________
An Act relative to civil rights and technology.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court assembled, and by the authority
of the same, as follows:
1 SECTION 1. Chapter 140 of the General Laws, as appearing in the 2022 Official Edition,
2 is hereby amended by inserting after section 131Y the following section:-
3 Section 131Z.
4 (a) As used in this section, the following words shall have the following meanings unless
5 the context clearly requires otherwise:
6 “Robotic device,” a device capable of locomotion, navigation, movement or flight that
7 operates at a distance from its operator or supervisor based on commands or in response to
8 sensor data, or a combination of both, including but not limited to an uncrewed aerial vehicle.
9 “Weapon”, any device designed to threaten or cause death, incapacitation or physical
10 injury to a person, including but not limited to firearms, chemical agents or irritants, kinetic
11 impact projectiles, weaponized lasers and explosive devices.
1 of 5
12 (b) It shall be unlawful for any person, whether or not acting under color of law, to
13 manufacture, modify, sell, transfer, possess or operate a robotic device equipped or mounted
14 with a weapon. Whoever knowingly violates the provisions of this subsection shall be punished
15 by imprisonment in the state prison for not less than 2½ years nor more than 5 years, or in a
16 house of correction for not less than 18 months nor more than 2 ½ years. The sentence imposed
17 shall not be reduced to less than 18 months, nor suspended, nor shall any person convicted under
18 this subsection be eligible for probation, parole, work release, or furlough or receive any
19 deduction from their sentence for good conduct until they shall have served 18 months of such
20 sentence; provided, however, that the commissioner of correction may on the recommendation of
21 the warden, superintendent, or other person in charge of a correctional institution, grant to an
22 offender committed under this subsection a temporary release in the custody of an officer of such
23 institution for the following purposes only: to attend the funeral of a relative; to visit a critically
24 ill relative; or to obtain emergency medical or psychiatric service unavailable at said institution.
25 Prosecutions commenced under this subsection shall neither be continued without a finding nor
26 placed on file. Whoever, after having been convicted of any of the offenses set forth in this
27 subsection, commits a second or subsequent offense set forth in this subsection, shall be
28 punished by imprisonment in the state prison for not less than 5 years nor more than 7 years; for
29 a third such offense, by imprisonment in the state prison for not less than 7 years nor more than
30 10 years; and for a fourth such offense, by imprisonment in the state prison for not less than 10
31 years nor more than 15 years. The sentence imposed upon a person, who after a conviction of an
32 offense under this subsection commits a second or subsequent offense under this subsection,
33 shall not be suspended, nor shall any person so sentenced be eligible for probation or receive any
34 deduction from his sentence for good conduct.
2 of 5
35 (c) It shall be unlawful for any person, whether or not acting under color of law, to use a
36 robotic device to: (A) threaten to commit a crime in violation of section 2 of chapter 275; (B)
37 criminally harass another person in violation of section 43A of chapter 265; or (C) physically
38 restrain or to attempt to physically restrain another person. Whoever knowingly violates the
39 provisions of this subsection shall be punished by imprisonment in a house of correction for not
40 more than 2½ years, by a fine of not more than $1,000 or by both such fine and imprisonment.
41 Whoever, after having been convicted of any of the offenses set forth in this subsection, commits
42 a second or subsequent offense set forth in this subsection, shall be punished by imprisonment in
43 a house of correction for not more than 2½ years or in a state prison for not more than 10 years,
44 by a fine of not more than $15,000 or by both such fine and imprisonment.
45 (d) This section shall not apply to:
46 (i) the United States Department of Defense, or any of its departments, agencies or units,
47 and the Massachusetts National Guard;
48 (ii) a defense industrial company with respect to robotic devices that are within the scope
49 of its contract with the department of defense;
50 (iii) a defense industrial company with respect to robotic devices that are within the scope
51 of its waiver obtained from the attorney general;
52 (iv) robotic devices within the scope of a waiver obtained from the attorney general
53 solely for the development or testing of technology intended to detect, prevent or mitigate the
54 unauthorized weaponization of robotic devices; or
3 of 5
55 (v) robotic devices within the scope of a waiver obtained from the attorney general solely
56 for educational or entertainment purposes.
57 (e) It shall not be a violation of this section for law enforcement agencies or officers, as
58 those terms are defined in section 1 of chapter 6E, acting in the public performance of their
59 duties to operate a robotic device equipped or mounted with a weapon or disrupter technology:
60 (i) to destroy, defuse or dispose of explosives or suspected explosives; (ii) for the destruction of
61 property when there is an imminent threat of death or serious bodily injury; or (iii) for
62 development, evaluation, testing, education or training relating to the uses permitted in (ii) and
63 (iii) of this subsection.
64 (f) A law enforcement agency shall be required to obtain a warrant, or other legally
65 required judicial authorization, prior to deploying a robotic device: (i) onto private property in
66 any situation in which a warrant would be required if the entry onto that property were made by
67 an officer; and (ii) to conduct surveillance or location tracking in any situation in which a
68 warrant or other legally required judicial authorization would be required if such surveillance or
69 tracking were conducted by an officer or other technology.
70 (g) Any individual may bring a civil action for damages and equitable relief, including
71 injunctive relief, resulting from a violation of this section or a regulation promulgated under this
72 section in any court of competent jurisdiction. A plaintiff who prevails in an action under this
73 section shall be entitled to an award of reasonable attorneys’ fees and costs incurred in
74 connection with said action.
75 (h) Each law enforcement agency shall document, as a public record, each time it uses a
76 robotic device quarterly to the executive office of public safety and security. Reported
4 of 5
77 information shall include: the date and time of the use; the scope, target and objective of the use;
78 whether the robotic device was equipped or mounted with a weapon; the permitted reason for
79 use; and whether a warrant or other legally required judicial authorization was obtained. The
80 executive office of public safety and security shall annually, not later than March 31, publicly
81 report this information on its website.
82 (i) The secretary of the executive office of public safety may promulgate rules and
83 regulations to carry out the provisions of this section, including rules and regulations related to
84 the permitted uses of robotic devices equipped or mounted with a weapon by law enforcement
85 set forth in subsection (e).
86 (j) The attorney general shall promulgate rules and regulations relating to the waivers
87 described in subsection (d).
5 of 5
