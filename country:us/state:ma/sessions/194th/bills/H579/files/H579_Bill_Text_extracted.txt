HOUSE . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . No. 579
By Representative Frost of Auburn (by request), a petition (accompanied by bill, House, No.
579) of Brittany Winsky relative to teaching certification for persons with certain disabilities.
Education.
[SIMILAR MATTER FILED IN PREVIOUS SESSION
SEE HOUSE, NO. 3784 OF 2023-2024.]
The Commonwealth of Massachusetts
_______________
In the One Hundred and Ninety-Fourth General Court
(2025-2026)
_______________
An Act relative to teaching certification.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court assembled, and by the authority
of the same, as follows:
1 SECTION 1. Section 38G of chapter 71 of the General Laws, as amended by chapter 154
2 of the acts of 2022, is hereby further amended by inserting after the fifteenth paragraph the
3 following paragraph:-
4 For a person who, because of a temporary or permanent disability arising from
5 intellectual, sensory, emotional, physical or environmental factors, or other specific learning
6 disabilities, is or would be unable to pass the written examination, the board shall create a
7 practical alternative assessment to the test requirement of the eligibility criteria for certification
8 as a provisional educator and certification as a provisional educator with advanced standing.
9 SECTION 2. This act shall take effect 180 days following its passage.
