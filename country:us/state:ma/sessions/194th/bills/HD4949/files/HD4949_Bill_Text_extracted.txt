HOUSE DOCKET, NO. 4949 FILED ON: 7/21/2025
HOUSE . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . No.
By Representative Xiarhos of Barnstable, a petition (subject to Joint Rule 12) of Steven George
Xiarhos relative to the use of navigational applications for travel routes for heavy motor vehicles.
Transportation.
The Commonwealth of Massachusetts
_______________
In the One Hundred and Ninety-Fourth General Court
(2025-2026)
_______________
An Act relative to navigation applications and the protection of local roadways and public safety
response.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court assembled, and by the authority
of the same, as follows:
1 SECTION 1. The General Laws, as appearing in the 2018 Official Edition, are hereby
2 amended by inserting after chapter 90J the following chapter:-
3 Section 1. Navigation Applications and Heavy Vehicle Routing.
4 (a) No person, firm, corporation, or operator of a digital navigation application or global
5 positioning system (GPS), including but not limited to mobile apps and commercial routing
6 software, shall propose or suggest travel routes for heavy motor vehicles that divert such vehicles
7 from primary or commuter roadways onto local municipal roads, where such diversion is likely
8 to:
9 (1) Increase traffic congestion beyond the road’s designed capacity;
10 (2) Undermine public safety by restricting or delaying access for emergency response
11 vehicles;
1 of 2
12 (3) Exceed the structural limitations or designated use of local infrastructure; or
13 (4) Negatively impact the quality of life in residential or tourism-heavy areas.
14 (b) The department of transportation shall develop regulations, in consultation with
15 municipal governments and public safety agencies, to implement this section, including
16 identifying priority areas for restricted routing and disseminating appropriate routing guidance to
17 navigation application providers.
18 (c) This section shall not apply to emergency vehicles, public works vehicles, or vehicles
19 rerouted due to road closures, accidents, or extreme weather, as determined by public safety
20 officials.
