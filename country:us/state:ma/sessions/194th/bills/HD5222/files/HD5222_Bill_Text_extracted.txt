HOUSE DOCKET, NO. 5222 FILED ON: 10/8/2025
HOUSE . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . No.
By Representative Xiarhos of Barnstable, a petition (subject to Joint Rule 12) of Steven George
Xiarhos and others for legislation to establish penalties for racing on public ways and unlawful
motor vehicle competitions. The Judiciary.
The Commonwealth of Massachusetts
_______________
In the One Hundred and Ninety-Fourth General Court
(2025-2026)
_______________
An Act relative to racing on public ways and penalties for unlawful motor vehicle competitions.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court assembled, and by the authority
of the same, as follows:
1 SECTION 1. Chapter 90 of the General Laws, as appearing in the 2024 Official Edition,
2 is hereby amended by inserting after section 24X the following new section:-
3 SECTION 24Y. Racing on public ways; penalties; impoundment; forfeiture
4 (a) As used in this section, the following words shall, unless the context clearly requires
5 otherwise, have the following meanings:
6 “Race”, the operation of one or more motor vehicles in competition, arising from a
7 challenge to demonstrate superiority of a motor vehicle or driver, either by prior arrangement or
8 in immediate response to another driver, in which the operator attempts to outgain, outdistance,
9 or prevent another motor vehicle from passing, or to arrive at a given destination ahead of
10 another vehicle.
1 of 4
11 “Drag race”, the operation of two or more motor vehicles from a point side-by-side at
12 accelerating speeds in a competitive attempt to outdistance each other, or over a common
13 selected course for the purpose of comparing relative speed or power of acceleration.
14 “Spectator”, any person who knowingly attends or views a drag race or race prohibited
15 under this section, when such presence is the result of an affirmative choice to attend or
16 participate.
17 “Public way”, any highway, roadway, bridge, tunnel, parking lot open to the public, or
18 any area maintained by a political subdivision of the Commonwealth for vehicular travel.
19 (b) No person shall:
20 (1) Operate a motor vehicle, including a motorcycle, in any race, speed competition, drag
21 race, acceleration contest, test of physical endurance, or exhibition of speed or acceleration on
22 any public way; (2) Coordinate, facilitate, or collect funds for any such event; (3) Knowingly
23 ride as a passenger in any such race or contest; or (4) Intentionally obstruct or slow the
24 movement of traffic for the purpose of conducting or observing any such race or exhibition.
25 (c) Any person who violates subsection (b) shall be punished by:
26 (1) For a first offense, by imprisonment in a house of correction for not more than 2½
27 years or by a fine of not less than $500 and not more than $1,000, or both, and the registrar shall
28 suspend such person’s driver’s license or right to operate for 1 year. (2) For a second offense
29 within five years, by imprisonment in a house of correction for not more than 2½ years or by a
30 fine of not less than $1,000 and not more than $3,000, or both, and the registrar shall suspend
31 such person’s driver’s license or right to operate for 2 years. (3) For a third or subsequent offense
2 of 4
32 within five years, by imprisonment in a house of correction for not more than 2½ years or by a
33 fine of not less than $2,000 and not more than $5,000, or both, and the registrar shall suspend
34 such person’s driver’s license or right to operate for 4 years.
35 (d) Any person who violates this section as a spectator shall be punished by a fine of not
36 more than $250 for a first offense and not more than $500 for a second or subsequent offense.
37 (e) A law enforcement officer having probable cause to believe a person has violated
38 subsection (b) may arrest such person without a warrant and may cause the motor vehicle
39 operated by such person to be impounded for up to 30 business days.
40 All fees and costs associated with towing and storage shall be borne by the registered
41 owner of the vehicle unless the court orders dismissal of the charge.
42 (f) Any motor vehicle used in violation of this section by a person who, within the
43 previous five years, has been convicted of a violation under this section may be subject to
44 forfeiture as provided in Chapter 276, Section 3, if the person so convicted is the owner of the
45 vehicle.
46 (g) This section shall not apply to:
47 (1) Licensed or duly authorized racetracks or drag strips; or (2) Areas designated by
48 municipal or state authorities specifically for organized, permitted events involving motor
49 vehicle exhibitions.
50 SECTION 2. The registrar of motor vehicles, in consultation with the secretary of public
51 safety and security, shall promulgate regulations necessary to implement the provisions of this
3 of 4
52 act, including uniform reporting procedures and notice to offenders regarding license suspension
53 and impoundment.
4 of 4
