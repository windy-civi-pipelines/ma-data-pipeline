HOUSE DOCKET, NO. 5223 FILED ON: 10/8/2025
HOUSE . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . No.
By Representative Xiarhos of Barnstable, a petition (subject to Joint Rule 12) of Steven George
Xiarhos and others relative to penalties for assault and battery on a police officer. The Judiciary.
The Commonwealth of Massachusetts
_______________
In the One Hundred and Ninety-Fourth General Court
(2025-2026)
_______________
An Act relative to penalties for assault and battery on a police officer.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court assembled, and by the authority
of the same, as follows:
1 SECTION 1. Chapter 265 of the General Laws, as appearing in the 2022 Official Edition,
2 is hereby amended by striking out section 13D and inserting in place thereof the following
3 section:-
4 SECTION 13D Assault and Battery Upon a Police Officer
5 Whoever commits an assault and battery upon a police officer, as defined in section 1 of
6 chapter 90C, who is engaged in the performance of their duties at the time of such assault and
7 battery, shall be punished by imprisonment in the state prison for not more than 5 years or by
8 imprisonment in a house of correction for not more than 2½ years, or by a fine of not more than
9 $10,000, or by both such fine and imprisonment.
10 No sentence imposed under this section shall be suspended, nor shall any person
11 convicted hereunder be eligible for probation, parole, furlough, or work release until such person
12 shall have served at least 90 days of said sentence.
1 of 2
13 A prosecution commenced under this section shall not be continued without a finding nor
14 placed on file.
15 For purposes of this section, “police officer” shall include any constable, sheriff, deputy
16 sheriff, state police trooper, municipal police officer, environmental police officer, university or
17 college police officer, or any other law enforcement officer empowered to make arrests under the
18 laws of the commonwealth.
2 of 2
