SENATE DOCKET, NO. 2057 FILED ON: 1/17/2025
SENATE . . . . . . . . . . . . . . No. 1127
By Mr. Gomez, a petition (accompanied by bill, Senate, No. 1127) of Adam Gomez, Robyn K.
Kennedy, Joanne M. Comerford, Mike Connolly and other members of the General Court for
legislation to ensure access to equitable representation in immigration proceedings by creating
the Immigrant Legal Defense Fund. The Judiciary.
The Commonwealth of Massachusetts
_______________
In the One Hundred and Ninety-Fourth General Court
(2025-2026)
_______________
An Act ensuring access to equitable representation in immigration proceedings.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court assembled, and by the authority
of the same, as follows:
1 SECTION 1. Chapter 29 of the Massachusetts General Laws is amended by adding the
2 following section:
3 Section 2EEEEEEE.
4 (a) There is hereby established on the books of the commonwealth a separate fund to be
5 known as the Immigrant Legal Defense Fund, hereinafter the fund, in which shall be credited
6 appropriations by the general court and any monies generated for the fund by corporations or
7 nonprofit entities. The purpose of the fund shall be to provide immigration legal services to
8 eligible immigrants and refugees who lack access to legal representation and who are facing
9 removal proceedings from the United States, especially those in federal immigration detention.
10 The fund shall be administered by the office for refugees and immigrants for disbursement
11 through a competitive grant program to providers of the aforementioned legal services. Funds
1 of 6
12 shall also be disbursed to support the work of a nonprofit organization designated by the
13 administrator, to be known as the Coordinator, to build and administer a statewide, integrated
14 system for immigrant legal representation. The administrator shall maintain the fund as a
15 separate fund. Interest earned by the fund shall be credited to the fund.
16 (b) For the purposes of this section, the following words shall have the following
17 meanings unless the context clearly requires otherwise:
18 “Accredited representative”, a non-attorney employee or volunteer of a qualifying
19 nonprofit organization who has been approved by the U.S. Department of Justice to provide
20 immigration legal advice and to represent clients before the U.S. Citizenship and Immigration
21 Services, immigration courts and the Board of Immigration Appeals.
22 “Administrator”, the office of refugees and immigrants within the executive office of
23 health and human services.
24 “Advisory Committee”, the committee established pursuant to subsection (f).
25 “Coordinator”, a nonprofit entity designated by the Administrator to administer a
26 statewide integrated navigation and representation system for immigrant legal defense.
27 “Covered individual”, an income-eligible individual in a covered proceeding without
28 access to immigration legal representation who is a resident or domiciliary of the commonwealth
29 or who has a nexus to the commonwealth.
30 “Covered proceeding”, a judicial or administrative proceeding to determine whether a
31 covered individual is subject to removal from the United States, or a related proceeding
2 of 6
32 including but not limited to a habeas corpus proceeding, federal appeal, or other civil legal
33 matter affecting a covered individual’s removal proceedings.
34 "Covered venue", (i) an immigration court anywhere in the United States where the case
35 of a covered individual is located; (ii) any tribunal located within the commonwealth, including
36 but not limited to family courts and federal district courts; (iii) with respect to the provision of
37 legal services in the context of expedited removals, any location within the borders of the
38 commonwealth where expedited removals are processed; (iv) the Board of Immigration Appeals;
39 (v) the United States Circuit Courts of Appeals; (vi) the United States Supreme Court; (vii)
40 United States Citizenship and Immigration Services; and (viii) another tribunal as determined by
41 the administrator or a designee of the administrator on a case-by-case basis.
42 “Culturally responsive legal services”, means legal services that are respectful of and
43 relevant to the beliefs, practices, cultures and linguistic needs of diverse consumer or client
44 populations and communities whose members identify as having particular cultural or linguistic
45 affiliations by virtue of their place of birth, ancestry or ethnic origin, religion, preferred language
46 or language spoken at home, and that have the capacity to respond to the issues of diverse
47 communities and to require knowledge and capacity at systemic, organizational, professional and
48 individual levels of intervention.
49 "Immigration legal services", individualized legal assistance in a single consultation or
50 ongoing legal representation that is provided by a legal services provider to a covered individual,
51 and all legal advice, advocacy, and assistance, including but not limited to social service
52 assistance associated with such service.
3 of 6
53 "Income-eligible individual", an individual who is deemed eligible for legal services in a
54 covered proceeding based on criteria developed or approved by the administrator whose annual
55 gross household income is not in excess of two hundred percent of the federal poverty guidelines
56 as updated periodically in the Federal Register by the United States Department of Health and
57 Human Services under the authority of 42 U.S.C. § 9902(2).
58 "Legal services provider", an individual, organization, or association that has the
59 expertise to provide immigration legal services and is approved by the coordinator to provide
60 such services.
61 “Nexus to the commonwealth”, detained or incarcerated in the commonwealth or subject
62 to a covered proceeding in the commonwealth; transferred by federal immigration authorities to
63 immigration detention in another state; or as otherwise determined by the administrator or
64 designee of the administrator.
65 (c) The administrator shall designate, and provide sufficient funds to, a coordinator to
66 develop and administer a statewide, integrated system for immigrant legal representation which
67 shall include, but not be limited to (i) providing logistical support and technical assistance to
68 grantees; (ii) screening immigrant applicants to determine income and program eligibility
69 according to criteria developed or approved by the administrator; (iii) providing mentoring and
70 training for attorneys, paralegals, and accredited representatives; and (iv) providing case
71 placement and referral services; provided, that the administrator shall ensure that the designated
72 coordinator has substantial experience providing culturally responsive legal services to
73 immigrants and refugees and administering legal services and caseloads.
4 of 6
74 (d) The administrator shall issue a request for proposals from and shall award grants to
75 nonprofit organizations, law firms, and qualified private attorneys with significant immigration
76 legal experience in order to provide culturally responsive legal representation to covered
77 individuals in covered proceedings, including but not limited to (i) community-based
78 organizations that employ experienced immigration attorneys, accredited representatives and
79 paralegals; and (ii) private attorneys with immigration legal experience; provided, that such
80 grants shall cover the cost of representation and as well as fees associated with filings,
81 interpretation, and related costs.
82 (e) The administrator shall require grant recipients to provide immigration legal services
83 without cost to covered individuals, subject to capacity and prioritize services for detained
84 individuals and individuals at imminent risk of deportation. Legal representation shall begin at
85 least before the covered individual’s first scheduled appearance in a covered proceeding, or as
86 soon as otherwise practicable.
87 (f) The administrator shall appoint an advisory committee with up to fifteen members
88 from diverse regions of the Commonwealth that shall include, but is not limited to, 1
89 representative of the Immigration Impact Unit of the Committee for Public Counsel Services; 1
90 representative of the Massachusetts Immigrant and Refugee Advocacy Coalition; 1
91 representative of the New England chapter of the American Immigration Lawyers Association; 1
92 representative from the Massachusetts Law Reform Institute; 1 representative from a
93 Massachusetts law school; and at least 3 representatives of community-based organizations
94 based in diverse regions of the commonwealth that directly serve impacted populations. The
95 advisory committee shall make recommendations regarding the coordination of services and
96 development of policies, training, and procedures necessary to effectuate the requirements of this
5 of 6
97 Act. Matters to be considered by the advisory committee may include, but are not limited to, the
98 prioritization of types of cases and covered venues, the extent of access to legal services in
99 covered venues, coordination of services, community engagement efforts, and access to
100 appropriate translation services for individuals with limited English proficiency. The advisory
101 committee shall be convened by the administrator on a regular basis, but no less than four times a
102 year.
103 (g) Annually, not later than October 1, the administrator shall conduct a review of the
104 fund and its activities, including but not limited to: (i) the sources and amounts of funds
105 received; (ii) the expenditures made from the fund and the purposes of such expenditures; (iii)
106 but not limited to: (i) the sources and amounts of funds received; (ii) the expenditures made from
107 the fund and the purposes of such expenditures; (iii) data on the number and type of cases
108 represented as well as any barriers or challenges experienced by grantees. This review shall be
109 included in a report and submitted to the clerks of the house of representatives and the senate,
110 and the joint committee on the judiciary.
111 SECTION 2. (a) The general court shall, subject to appropriation, fund the Immigrant
112 Legal Defense Fund in the general appropriations act through a specifically dedicated line-item
113 to be administered by the office for refugees and immigrants within the executive office of health
114 and human services to effectuate the purposes of this act. This will not be in lieu of any
115 additional appropriation attributed to the fund through alternative sources or deposits.
116 (b) On or before July 31, 2026, and on July 31 each year thereafter, the administrator
117 shall award grants from the fund, subject to appropriation, to qualifying organizations.
6 of 6
