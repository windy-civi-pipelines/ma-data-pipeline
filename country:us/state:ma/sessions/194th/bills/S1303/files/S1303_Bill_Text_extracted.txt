SENATE DOCKET, NO. 150 FILED ON: 1/8/2025
SENATE . . . . . . . . . . . . . . No. 1303
By Ms. Edwards, a petition (accompanied by bill, Senate, No. 1303) of Lydia Edwards, Paul W.
Mark and Vanna Howard for legislation to provide opportunities for apprentices to complete
their training and ensuring for a skilled workforce in the Commonwealth of Massachusetts.
Labor and Workforce Development.
[SIMILAR MATTER FILED IN PREVIOUS SESSION
SEE SENATE, NO. 1163 OF 2023-2024.]
The Commonwealth of Massachusetts
_______________
In the One Hundred and Ninety-Fourth General Court
(2025-2026)
_______________
An Act providing opportunities for apprentices to complete their training and ensuring for a
skilled workforce in the commonwealth of Massachusetts.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court assembled, and by the authority
of the same, as follows:
1 Section 26 of Chapter 149 is hereby amended by adding at the end:
2 For projects over one million dollars, the total hours of employees, by trade, receiving an
3 hourly wage who are directly employed on the site of the project, employed by the contractor or
4 a subcontractor and subject to the prevailing wage, shall be performed by apprentices in bona
5 fide apprentice training programs as provided in sections 11H and 11I of chapter 23 of the
6 General Laws which are approved by the division of apprentice training in the executive office
7 of labor and workforce development and in compliance with apprentice ratios established by any
8 trades license board.
1 of 3
9 Six months after the passage of this act 5% of the hours shall be performed by
10 apprentices.
11 One year after 10%
12 Two years after 15%
13 Section 6 of Chapter 149 A shall be amended by adding a new section:
14 (f) For projects over one million dollars, the total hours of employees, by trade, receiving
15 an hourly wage who are directly employed on the site of the project, employed by the contractor
16 or a subcontractor and subject to the prevailing wage, shall be performed by apprentices in bona
17 fide apprentice training programs as provided in sections 11H and 11I of chapter 23 of the
18 General Laws which are approved by the division of apprentice training in the executive office
19 of labor and workforce development and in compliance with apprentice ratios established by any
20 trades license board.
21 Six months after the passage of this act 5% of the hours shall be performed by
22 apprentices.
23 One year after 10%
24 Two years after 15%
25 Chapter 30 Section 39m is hereby amended by adding a new section:
26 (f) For projects over one million dollars, the total hours of employees, by trade, receiving
27 an hourly wage who are directly employed on the site of the project, employed by the contractor
28 or a subcontractor and subject to the prevailing wage, shall be performed by apprentices in bona
2 of 3
29 fide apprentice training programs as provided in sections 11H and 11I of chapter 23 of the
30 General Laws which are approved by the division of apprentice training in the executive office
31 of labor and workforce development and in compliance with apprentice ratios established by any
32 trades license board.
33 Six months after the passage of this act 5% of the hours shall be performed by
34 apprentices.
35 One year after 10%
36 Two years after 15%
