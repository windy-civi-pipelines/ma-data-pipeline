SENATE DOCKET, NO. 102 FILED ON: 1/7/2025
SENATE . . . . . . . . . . . . . . No. 1588
By Mr. Moore, a petition (accompanied by bill, Senate, No. 1588) of Michael O. Moore and
James B. Eldridge for legislation relative to chemicals in food packaging. Public Health.
[SIMILAR MATTER FILED IN PREVIOUS SESSION
SEE SENATE, NO. 1431 OF 2023-2024.]
The Commonwealth of Massachusetts
_______________
In the One Hundred and Ninety-Fourth General Court
(2025-2026)
_______________
An Act relative to chemicals in food packaging.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court assembled, and by the authority
of the same, as follows:
1 SECTION 1. Chapter 94B of the General Laws, as appearing in the 2022 Official
2 Edition, is hereby amended by inserting after section 10 the following section:-
3 Section 11. (a) As used in this section, the following words shall have the following
4 meanings:-
5 “Food package", a package or packaging component that is intended for the marketing,
6 protection or handling of a product intended for food contact or used to store food and foodstuffs
7 for sale.
8 "Manufacturer", a person, firm, association, partnership, government entity, organization,
9 joint venture or corporation that applies a package to a product for distribution or sale.
1 of 3
10 "Package", a container providing a means of marketing, protecting, or handling a product
11 which shall include a unit package, an intermediate package, a package used for shipping or
12 transport and unsealed receptacles such as carrying cases, crates, cups, pails, rigid foil and other
13 trays, wrappers and wrapping films, bags, and tubs.
14 "Packaging component", an individual assembled part of a package including, but not
15 limited to, any interior or exterior blocking, bracing, cushioning, weatherproofing, exterior
16 strapping, coatings, closures, inks and labels.
17 "Perfluoroalkyl and polyfluoroalkyl substances", a class of fluorinated organic chemicals
18 containing at least one fully fluorinated carbon atom.
19 (b) No person or entity shall manufacture, knowingly sell, offer for sale, distribute for
20 sale or distribute for use in the commonwealth food packaging to which perfluoroalkyl and
21 polyfluoroalkyl substances have been intentionally added in any amount.
22 (c) A certificate of compliance stating that a package or packaging component is in
23 compliance with the requirements of this section shall be furnished by its manufacturer or
24 supplier to its purchaser. The certificate of compliance shall be signed by an authorized officer of
25 the manufacturing or supplying company. The purchaser shall retain the certificate of
26 compliance for as long as the package or packaging component is in use. A copy of the
27 certificate of compliance shall be kept on file by the manufacturer or supplier of the package or
28 packaging component. Certificates of compliance, or copies thereof, shall be furnished to the
29 department of public health upon request and to members of the public in accordance with
30 section 9.
2 of 3
31 If the manufacturer or supplier of the package or packaging component reformulates or
32 creates a new package or packaging component, the manufacturer or supplier shall provide an
33 amended or new certificate of compliance for the reformulated or new package or packaging
34 component.
35 SECTION 2. Subsection (b) of section 1 shall take effect on January 1, 2027.
36 SECTION 3. Subsection (c) of said section 1 shall take effect 90 days after the effective
37 date of this act.
3 of 3
