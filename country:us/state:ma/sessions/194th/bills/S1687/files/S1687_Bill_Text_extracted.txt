SENATE DOCKET, NO. 884 FILED ON: 1/15/2025
SENATE . . . . . . . . . . . . . . No. 1687
By Mr. Fattman, a petition (accompanied by bill, Senate, No. 1687) of Ryan C. Fattman and
Alyson M. Sullivan-Almeida for legislation relative to the lawful sale of ammunition. Public
Safety and Homeland Security.
[SIMILAR MATTER FILED IN PREVIOUS SESSION
SEE SENATE, NO. 1516 OF 2023-2024.]
The Commonwealth of Massachusetts
_______________
In the One Hundred and Ninety-Fourth General Court
(2025-2026)
_______________
An Act relative to the lawful sale of ammunition.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court assembled, and by the authority
of the same, as follows:
1 Section 122B of chapter 140 of the General Laws, as appearing in the 2022 Official
2 Edition, shall be amended by inserting the following language at the end of the section:-
3 Notwithstanding any special law or regulation to the contrary, nothing in this section
4 shall be deemed as requiring a company outside of the Commonwealth to be required to be
5 licensed under this section. Said companies shall be entitled to sell and deliver inert ammunition
6 components to persons properly licensed to possess or purchase ammunition under sections 129B
7 or 131 of this chapter, through the internet, U.S. Mail or by any other means common to
8 interstate commerce. All deliveries of ammunition under this paragraph, whether in person or by
9 common carrier, must require the signature of the licensee and photographic identification.
1 of 2
10 For the purposes of this section the term "inert components" shall include, but not be
11 limited to the following: cases, bullets, shot, wads.
2 of 2
