Patricia D. Jehlen

Court assembled:
The undersigned legislators and/or citizens respectfully petition for the adoption of the accompanying bill:
An Act relative to the sustainability of the family childcare sector.
_______________
PETITION OF:
NAME: DISTRICT/ADDRESS:
Patricia D. Jehlen Second Middlesex
1 of 1
SENATE DOCKET, NO. 1442 FILED ON: 1/16/2025
SENATE . . . . . . . . . . . . . . No. 2019
By Ms. Jehlen, a petition (accompanied by bill, Senate, No. 2019) of Patricia D. Jehlen for
legislation relative to certain child-care deductions for taxable purposes. Revenue.
The Commonwealth of Massachusetts
_______________
In the One Hundred and Ninety-Fourth General Court
(2025-2026)
_______________
An Act relative to the sustainability of the family childcare sector.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court assembled, and by the authority
of the same, as follows:
1 Section 6 of Chapter 62 of the General Laws is hereby amended by adding after
2 subsection X the following new section:-
3 Section (y)
4 (1) In addition to any other credit allowed pursuant to this chapter, a taxpayer who
5 provides Family child care services may be allowed a credit equal to $3300 adjusted to the
6 consumer price index starting with the year that the credit is applicable to the person’s tax filing.
7 Any Family child care provider that receives a credit pursuant to this section for any taxable year
8 may carry over and apply to its tax due for any 1 or more of the next succeeding 3 taxable years
9 the portion, as reduced from year to year, of the credit that exceeds any tax due for the taxable
10 year.
1 of 2
11 (2) For the purposes of subsection (y), paragraph (1), “Family child care provider” and
12 "Family child care services” shall have the same meaning as under subsection (a) of Section 17
13 of Chapter 15D of the General Laws.
2 of 2
