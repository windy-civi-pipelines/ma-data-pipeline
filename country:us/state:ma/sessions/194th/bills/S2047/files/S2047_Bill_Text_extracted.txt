SENATE DOCKET, NO. 1027 FILED ON: 1/15/2025
SENATE . . . . . . . . . . . . . . No. 2047
By Mr. Moore, a petition (accompanied by bill, Senate, No. 2047) of Michael O. Moore for
legislation to reform the healthcare cost benchmark. Revenue.
The Commonwealth of Massachusetts
_______________
In the One Hundred and Ninety-Fourth General Court
(2025-2026)
_______________
An Act to reform the healthcare cost benchmark.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court assembled, and by the authority
of the same, as follows:
1 SECTION 1. Section 7H 1/2 of chapter 29 of the General Laws, as so appearing, is
2 hereby amended by inserting after the definition of “Growth rate of potential gross state product”
3 the following definition:-
4 “Historical growth rate in gross state product”, the long-run average annual growth rate
5 of the commonwealth's gross state product measured over the most recent ten-year period.
6 SECTION 2. Section 7H 1/2 of chapter 29 of the General Laws, as so appearing, is
7 hereby amended by striking the last sentence in section (b).
8 SECTION 3. Section 7H 1/2 of chapter 29 of the General Laws, as so appearing, is
9 hereby amended by inserting the following new section:-
10 (d) On or before January 15, the secretary of administration and finance, in consultation
11 with the house and senate committees on ways and means, shall calculate the average annual
12 growth rate of actual gross state product for the most recent ten-year period. The secretary shall
1 of 2
13 report to the house and senate ways and means committees its methodology and results. The
14 historical growth rate in gross state product shall be provided to the health policy commission
15 established under chapter 6D and be made public by the executive office of administration and
16 finance.
17 SECTION 4. Section 9 of Chapter 6D is hereby further amended by striking subsection
18 (b)(3) in its entirety and replacing with the following:-
19 (3) For calendar years 2023 and beyond, the health care cost growth benchmark shall be
20 equal to historical growth rate in gross state product established under said section 7H 1/2 of
21 chapter 29 of the General Laws.
