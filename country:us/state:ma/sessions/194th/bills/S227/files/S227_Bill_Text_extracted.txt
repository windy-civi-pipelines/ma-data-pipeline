SENATE DOCKET, NO. 942 FILED ON: 1/15/2025
SENATE . . . . . . . . . . . . . . No. 227
By Mr. Fattman, a petition (accompanied by bill, Senate, No. 227) of Ryan C. Fattman and
Bruce E. Tarr for legislation to expand options for EMT paramedics. Consumer Protection and
Professional Licensure.
[SIMILAR MATTER FILED IN PREVIOUS SESSION
SEE SENATE, NO. 163 OF 2023-2024.]
The Commonwealth of Massachusetts
_______________
In the One Hundred and Ninety-Fourth General Court
(2025-2026)
_______________
An Act expanding options for EMT paramedics.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court assembled, and by the authority
of the same, as follows:
1 SECTION 1. Notwithstanding any general or special law to the contrary, any emergency
2 medical technician, as defined by section 1 of chapter 111C of the General Laws, who has been
3 (i) awarded a paramedic certification from the National Registry of Emergency Medical
4 Technicians, (ii) awarded an EMT-Paramedic certificate as administered by the department of
5 public health, (iii) completed no less than 9,000 hours of paramedic field work for any licensed
6 ambulance service in the commonwealth after receiving said certificates, and has (iv) completed
7 a National Council Licensure Exam (NCLEX) approved preparation course, shall not be required
8 to graduate from a Board of Registration in Nursing approved nursing program in the
9 commonwealth to participate in the NCLEX-RN.
1 of 2
10 SECTION 2. Any emergency medical technician, as defined in section 1 of chapter 111C
11 of the General Laws, who has been (i) awarded an associate of science, bachelor of science, or
12 master of science in emergency medical services at an accredited college or university, (ii)
13 awarded a paramedic certification from the National Registry of Emergency Medical
14 Technicians, (iii) awarded an EMT-Paramedic certificate as administered by the department of
15 public health, (iv) completed no less than 5,000 hours of paramedic field work for any licensed
16 ambulance service in the commonwealth after receiving said certificates, and has (v) completed a
17 National Council Licensure Exam (NCLEX) approved preparation course, shall not be required
18 to graduate from a Board of Registration in Nursing approved nursing program in the
19 commonwealth to participate in the NCLEX-RN.
20 SECTION 3. Any emergency medical technician paramedic shall be required to provide
21 specific documentation for Good Moral Character (GMC) evaluation as defined in sections 74,
22 74A, and 76 of chapter 112 of the General Laws to be licensed as a registered nurse in the
23 commonwealth.
24 SECTION 4. The department of public health shall develop regulations and guidelines
25 within 180 days of the passage of this act.
2 of 2
