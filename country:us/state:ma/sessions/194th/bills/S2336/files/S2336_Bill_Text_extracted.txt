Court assembled:
The undersigned legislators and/or citizens respectfully petition for the adoption of the accompanying
resolve:
Resolve relative to State House restoration and management commission.
_______________
PETITION OF:
NAME: DISTRICT/ADDRESS:
Jacob R. Oliveira Hampden, Hampshire and Worcester
1 of 1
SENATE DOCKET, NO. 543 FILED ON: 1/13/2025
SENATE . . . . . . . . . . . . . . No. 2336
By Mr. Oliveira, a petition (accompanied by resolve, Senate, No. 2336) of Jacob R. Oliveira that
provisions be made for an investigation and study by a special commission (including members
of the General Court) to establish a State House restoration and management commission.
Tourism, Arts and Cultural Development.
[SIMILAR MATTER FILED IN PREVIOUS SESSION
SEE SENATE, NO. 2195 OF 2023-2024.]
The Commonwealth of Massachusetts
_______________
In the One Hundred and Ninety-Fourth General Court
(2025-2026)
_______________
Resolve relative to State House restoration and management commission.
1 Resolved, There shall be a special commission to formulate and develop a master plan
2 and program to restore and preserve the Massachusetts State House and Grounds.
3 The commission shall consist of the following members: the president of the senate or
4 designee, the speaker of the house or designee, the house and senate minority leaders or
5 designees, the executive director for the Massachusetts Office of Travel and Tourism, the
6 president of the Boston Society for Architecture, one member with experience in historic
7 preservation shall be appointed by the governor, one member with experience in historic
8 preservation shall be appointed by the secretary of the commonwealth, one member with
9 experience in historic preservation shall be appointed by the secretary of Housing and Economic
10 Development.
1 of 2
11 The commission shall establish the following (a) policy controlling the furnishings,
12 including, but not limited to, wall, floor, and window coverings of the State House; (b) policy
13 governing the maintenance of the State House; (c) policy governing the use of the State House
14 for any nongovernmental activities; (d) policy relative to historical commemorative additions
15 and improvements on such as statues and monuments, (e) approve such historical
16 commemorative additions and improvements as may come with such policy; (f) establish a
17 tourism plan for the State House.
18 The commission will provide a report by January 2026 with findings and
19 recommendations of the overall management and restoration of the State House.
2 of 2
