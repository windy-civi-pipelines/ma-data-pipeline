SENATE DOCKET, NO. 371 FILED ON: 1/13/2025
SENATE . . . . . . . . . . . . . . No. 2450
By Mr. Tarr, a petition (accompanied by bill, Senate, No. 2450) of Bruce E. Tarr for legislation
to protect the health and well-being of child passengers in vehicles. Transportation.
[SIMILAR MATTER FILED IN PREVIOUS SESSION
SEE SENATE, NO. 2300 OF 2023-2024.]
The Commonwealth of Massachusetts
_______________
In the One Hundred and Ninety-Fourth General Court
(2025-2026)
_______________
An Act to protect the health and well-being of child passengers in vehicles.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court assembled, and by the authority
of the same, as follows:
1 SECTION 1. Section 7AA of chapter 90, as appearing in the 2022 Official Edition, is
2 hereby amended by striking out the second sentence of paragraph 3 and replacing it with the
3 following sentence:-
4 An operator of a motor vehicle who violates the provisions of this section shall be subject
5 to a fine of not more than one hundred dollars; provided, however, that said one hundred dollar
6 fine shall not apply to an operator of a motor vehicle licensed as a taxi cab not equipped with a
7 child passenger restraint device.
8 SECTION 2. Section 13A of chapter 90, as appearing in the 2022 Official Edition, is
9 hereby amended by striking out the sentence "Any operator of a motor vehicle shall be subject to
10 an additional fine of twenty-five dollars for each person under the age of sixteen and no younger
1 of 2
11 than twelve who is a passenger in said motor vehicle and not wearing a safety belt." and
12 replacing it with the following sentence:-
13 Any operator of a motor vehicle shall be subject to an additional fine of one hundred
14 dollars for each person under the age of sixteen who is a passenger in said motor vehicle and not
15 wearing a safety belt.
16 SECTION 3. The registrar, as defined in Section 56 of chapter 6C shall approve driver
17 education information relative to the health and safety risks of unsecured children in vehicles for
18 publication in the Registry of Motor Vehicles' Driver’s Manual.
