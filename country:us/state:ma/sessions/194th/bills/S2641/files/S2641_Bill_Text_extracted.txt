SENATE . . . . . . . . . . . . . . No. 2641
The Commonwealth of Massachusetts
_______________
In the One Hundred and Ninety-Fourth General Court
(2025-2026)
_______________
An Act relative to menstrual product ingredient disclosure.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court assembled, and by the authority
of the same, as follows:
1 SECTION 1. Chapter 94 of the General Laws is hereby amended by adding the following
2 section:-
3 Section 330. (a) For the purposes of this section, the following words shall have the
4 following meanings unless the context clearly requires otherwise:
5 “Ingredient”, an intentionally added substance present in a menstrual product.
6 “Manufacturer”, (i) a person or entity that manufactures a menstrual product and whose
7 name appears on the product label; or (ii) a person or entity for whom the menstrual product is
8 manufactured or distributed, as identified on the product label pursuant to 15 U.S.C. section 1451
9 et seq.
10 “Menstrual product”, a product used to collect menstruation and vaginal discharge
11 including, but not limited to, tampons, pads, menstrual cups, disks, sponges and menstrual
12 underwear, whether disposable or reusable.
1 of 2
13 (b) A package or box containing menstrual products manufactured for sale or distribution
14 in the commonwealth shall have a label, printed or affixed thereto, that includes a plain and
15 conspicuous list of all the product’s ingredients in order of predominance.
16 (c) A manufacturer of a menstrual product that is manufactured for sale or distribution in
17 the commonwealth shall post on their internet website, in an electronically readable format, the
18 ingredient information that is required to be disclosed pursuant to subsection (b).
19 (d) If there is a change or addition of an ingredient to a menstrual product, the
20 manufacturer shall update: (i) the label on such menstrual product box or package not later than
21 18 months after the change or addition of said ingredient; and (ii) the ingredient information
22 disclosed on the internet website pursuant to subsection (c) not later than 2 months after the
23 change or addition of the ingredient.
24 (e) A manufacturer in violation of this section shall be punished by a fine of not more
25 than $1,000 dollars per violation.
26 SECTION 2. This act shall take effect 12 months after its passage.
2 of 2
