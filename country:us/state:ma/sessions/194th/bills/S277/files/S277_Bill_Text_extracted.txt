SENATE DOCKET, NO. 1105 FILED ON: 1/15/2025
SENATE . . . . . . . . . . . . . . No. 277
By Mr. Oliveira, a petition (accompanied by bill, Senate, No. 277) of Jacob R. Oliveira for
legislation to regulate the practice and licensure of veterinary technicians. Consumer Protection
and Professional Licensure.
[SIMILAR MATTER FILED IN PREVIOUS SESSION
SEE HOUSE, NO. 4912 OF 2023-2024.]
The Commonwealth of Massachusetts
_______________
In the One Hundred and Ninety-Fourth General Court
(2025-2026)
_______________
An Act regulating the practice and licensure of veterinary technicians.
Whereas, The deferred operation of this act would tend to defeat its purpose, which is to
forthwith regulate the practice and licensure of veterinary technicians, therefore it is hereby
declared to be an emergency law, necessary for the immediate preservation of the public
convenience.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court assembled, and by the authority
of the same, as follows:
1 SECTION 1. Chapter 13 of the General Laws is hereby amended by striking out section
2 26 and inserting in place thereof the following section:-
3 Section 26. (a) There shall be a board of registration in veterinary medicine, in sections
4 27 and 28 called the board, consisting of 4 veterinarians, 3 licensed veterinary technicians, as
5 defined in section 54A of chapter 112 and 2 representatives of the public, subject to section 9B.
6 Each member shall be appointed by the governor. Each member of the board shall be a legal
7 resident of the commonwealth. Each veterinarian member of the board shall be licensed to
1 of 5
8 practice veterinary medicine in the commonwealth, a graduate of a school or college teaching
9 veterinary medicine and approved by the approving authority and shall have had at least 5 years’
10 experience in the active practice of veterinary medicine prior to their appointment. Each licensed
11 veterinary technician member shall be licensed in accordance with section 56F of chapter 112
12 and have at least 5 years experience in the active practice of veterinary technology. No member
13 shall be a member of any faculty in any institution having the power to confer degrees in
14 veterinary medicine. One member thereof shall annually before December first be appointed by
15 the governor, for 5 years from January first following. No member appointed to a full 5-year
16 term shall be reappointed to succeed themself.
17 SECTION 2. Section 54A of chapter 112 of the General Laws, as appearing in the 2022
18 Official Edition, is hereby amended by inserting, after the definition of “Board”, the following
19 definition:-
20 “Licensed veterinary technician” and “veterinary technician,” a graduate of an American
21 Veterinary Medical Association accredited program in veterinary technology or veterinary
22 nursing who has achieved a passing score on an approved licensing exam as determined by the
23 board and is licensed in the commonwealth.
24 SECTION 3. Said section 54A of said chapter 112, as so appearing, is hereby further
25 amended by inserting, after the definition of “Veterinarian,” the following definition:-
26 “Veterinary assistant,” an individual working as part of a veterinary healthcare team who
27 is not a licensed veterinary technician.
28 SECTION 4. Said section 54A of said chapter 112, as so appearing, is hereby further
29 amended by adding the following definition:-
2 of 5
30 “Veterinary technology,” the performance of services within the field of veterinary
31 medicine by a licensed veterinary technician who is employed by or under the supervision of a
32 licensed veterinarian to perform such duties as are required in carrying out medical orders as
33 prescribed by a licensed veterinarian.
34 SECTION 5. Said chapter 112 is hereby further amended by inserting after section 56E
35 the following section:-
36 Section 56F. (a)(1) An individual shall be licensed as a veterinary technician, by the
37 board, to practice veterinary technology. Only those individuals who are licensed shall utilize the
38 title of veterinary technician. No person shall hold themself out as a licensed veterinary
39 technician in the commonwealth unless licensed in accordance with this section; provided, that
40 any individual or veterinary practice in violation of this subsection may be subject to fines as
41 established by the board.
42 (2) Veterinary technician students, or other individuals deemed appropriate by the board,
43 working under the supervision of a licensed veterinarian or a licensed veterinary technician may
44 engage in activities necessary to learn and practice supervised techniques on patients.
45 (b)(1) The board shall promulgate rules and regulations by which veterinary technicians
46 shall be licensed and registered in the commonwealth. Any rules that allow individuals to
47 become licensed without a formal degree in veterinary technology shall have an end date no later
48 than July 1, 2030. The board may recommend the suspension or revocation of a veterinary
49 technician’s license for failure to abide by the laws of the commonwealth or regulations
50 promulgated by the board.
3 of 5
51 (2) The regulations shall: (i) define the duties which supervising, licensed veterinarians
52 may assign to licensed veterinary technicians and to non-licensed veterinary assistants; (ii)
53 address the maintenance, suspension and revocation of licenses; (iii) provide for continuing
54 education requirements for licensed veterinary technicians; (iv) determine the criteria by which
55 an individual shall be deemed a licensed veterinary technician or a veterinary assistant; and (v)
56 establish guidance for students working under the supervision of a licensed veterinarian or
57 licensed veterinarian technician pursuant to paragraph (2) of subsection (a).
58 (c) This section shall not apply to: (i) a research institution as defined in section 136A of
59 chapter 140; or (ii) special cases, including zoos and aquaria, diagnostic facilities, licensed
60 wildlife rehabilitation facilities and any other such special cases as the board shall identify.
61 SECTION 6. Notwithstanding section 26 of chapter 13 of the General Laws, or any other
62 general or special law to the contrary, the initial appointments of veterinary technicians to the
63 board of registration in veterinary medicine shall be Massachusetts Veterinary Technician
64 Association certified veterinary technicians selected from a list of nominees provided to the
65 governor. For the initial appointments of veterinary technicians, there shall be one 3 year
66 appointment, one 4 year appointment, and one 5 year appointment.
67 SECTION 7. The board of registration in veterinary medicine shall promulgate
68 regulations pursuant to section 56F of chapter 112 of the General Laws, inserted by section 5, not
69 later than 180 days after the effective date of this act.
70 SECTION 8. Not later than 180 days after the effective date of this act, the board of
71 registration in veterinary medicine shall provide information to veterinarians, veterinary
72 technicians, veterinary practices and animal hospitals regarding definitions of roles of licensed
4 of 5
73 veterinary technicians and veterinary assistants as promulgated under regulations pursuant to
74 section 56F of chapter 112 of the General Laws, inserted by section 5.
75 SECTION 9. The board of registration in veterinary medicine shall issue temporary
76 licenses for all current certified veterinary technicians through the Massachusetts Veterinary
77 Technician Association and shall promulgate regulations for the temporary licensure of
78 individuals practicing veterinary technology within 180 days of the effective date of this act.
79 SECTION 10. All individuals practicing veterinary medicine technology required to be
80 licensed pursuant to section 56F of chapter 112 of the General Laws, inserted by section 5, shall
81 be licensed not later than July 1, 2030.
5 of 5
