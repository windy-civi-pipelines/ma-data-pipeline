SENATE DOCKET, NO. 213 FILED ON: 1/9/2025
SENATE . . . . . . . . . . . . . . No. 453
By Mr. Tarr, a petition (accompanied by bill, Senate, No. 453) of Bruce E. Tarr and Mehak
Sankhla, for legislation relative to student absences based on religiously held beliefs.
Education.
The Commonwealth of Massachusetts
_______________
In the One Hundred and Ninety-Fourth General Court
(2025-2026)
_______________
An Act relative to student absences based on religiously held beliefs.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court assembled, and by the authority
of the same, as follows:
1 SECTION 1. Chapter 71 of the General Laws, as appearing in the 2022 Official Edition,
2 is hereby amended by inserting at the end the following:-
3 Section __: Notwithstanding section 31A, no student shall be punished for an absence
4 when such absence is the result of the observance of a meaningfully held religious belief.
5 SECTION 2. This act shall take effect upon its passage.
