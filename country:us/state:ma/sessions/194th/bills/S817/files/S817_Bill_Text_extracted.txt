An Act to ensure access to epinephrine for minors.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court assembled, and by the authority
of the same, as follows:
1 SECTION 1. Chapter 32A of the General Laws, as appearing in the 2022 Official
2 Edition, is hereby amended by inserting after section 17Q the following section:-
3 Section 17R. (a) Any coverage offered by the commission to an active or retired
4 employee of the commonwealth insured under the group insurance commission shall provide
5 coverage at no cost for medically necessary epinephrine injectors for individuals under 18 years
6 of age for the appropriate weight-based dosage of epinephrine injectors.
7 (b) For purposes of this section, the term “epinephrine injector” shall mean an auto-
8 injector approved by the United States Food and Drug Administration for the administration of
9 epinephrine and a pre-filled syringe approved by the United States Food and Drug
1 of 6
10 Administration and used for the administration of epinephrine that contains a pre-measured dose
11 of epinephrine that is equivalent to the dosages used in an auto-injector.
12 (c) Coverage for epinephrine auto-injector 2-packs, under this section, shall not be
13 subject to any deductible, co-insurance, or co-payment that exceeds $60 per supply, regardless of
14 the amount or type of epinephrine needed to fill an insured person’s prescription for those 18
15 years of age and older.
16 SECTION 2. (a) Chapter 118E of the General Laws is hereby amended by inserting after
17 section 10L the following section:-
18 Section 10M. The division and its contracted health insurers, health plans, health
19 maintenance organizations, behavioral health management firms and third-party administrators
20 under contract to a Medicaid managed care organization or primary care clinician plan shall
21 provide coverage at no cost for medically necessary epinephrine injectors for individuals under
22 18 years of age for the appropriate weight-based dosage of epinephrine injectors.
23 (b) For purposes of this section, the term “epinephrine injector” shall mean an auto-
24 injector approved by the United States Food and Drug Administration for the administration of
25 epinephrine and a pre-filled syringe approved by the United States Food and Drug
26 Administration and used for the administration of epinephrine that contains a pre-measured dose
27 of epinephrine that is equivalent to the dosages used in an auto-injector.
28 (c) Coverage for epinephrine auto-injector 2-packs, under this section, shall not be
29 subject to any deductible, co-insurance, or co-payment that exceeds $60 per supply, regardless of
30 the amount or type of epinephrine needed to fill an insured person’s prescription for those 18
31 years of age and older.

91 SECTION 7. (a) There shall be a commission within the executive office of health and
92 human services for the purpose of studying and making recommendations relative to the
93 manufacturing and pricing of dupilumab, omalizumab, and allergy immunotherapy shots,
94 specifically the creation of generic versions.
5 of 6
95 (b) The commission shall consist of members from the secretary of health and human
96 services, department of public health, center for health information and analysis, and the division
97 of insurance.
98 (c) Members of the commission shall be appointed within 30 days of this act being
99 effective. The commission shall meet not fewer than 6 times per calendar year.
100 (d) The commission shall submit an annual report by December 31 to the governor, the
101 clerks of the house of representatives and the senate, and the joint committee on consumer
102 protection and professional licensure. The report will be a public record.
