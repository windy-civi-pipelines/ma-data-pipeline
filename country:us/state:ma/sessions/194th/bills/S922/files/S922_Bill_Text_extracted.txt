SENATE DOCKET, NO. 766 FILED ON: 1/14/2025
SENATE . . . . . . . . . . . . . . No. 922
By Ms. Creem, a petition (accompanied by bill, Senate, No. 922) of Cynthia Stone Creem for
legislation relative to plant-based protein. Higher Education.
The Commonwealth of Massachusetts
_______________
In the One Hundred and Ninety-Fourth General Court
(2025-2026)
_______________
An Act relative to the Bean New Deal.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Court assembled, and by the authority
of the same, as follows:
1 SECTION 1. Chapter 7C of the General Laws is hereby amended by inserting the
2 following new section:-
3 Section 74. The division shall ensure that all dining halls, cafeterias, canteens and similar
4 facilities that are owned, operated or contracted by public institutions of higher education or
5 executive offices, departments, divisions, centers, agencies or authorities of the commonwealth
6 shall offer one or more plant-based protein sources at every meal they serve; provided, however,
7 that this section shall not apply to facilities that are under contract prior to the effective date of
8 this section until the contract expires or is terminated, renegotiated or renewed.
9 SECTION 2. Chapter 15A of the General Laws is hereby amended by inserting after
10 section 47 the following new section:-
11 Section 48. All culinary arts degree and certificate programs offered by public institutions
12 of higher education shall provide instruction concerning: (i) the health, climate, and
1 of 5
13 environmental benefits of maintaining a diet that involves protein sources that are exclusively,
14 predominantly or partially plant-based; and (ii) how to integrate plant-based protein sources into
15 a healthy diet and prepare healthy meals that involve protein sources that are exclusively,
16 predominantly or partially plant-based.
17 SECTION 3. Chapter 19A of the General Laws is hereby amended by inserting the
18 following new section:-
19 Section 45. In administering senior nutrition programs, including the hot lunch program
20 described in section 37, the department shall ensure that every program participant receiving
21 home-delivered meals has the ability to request and receive meals that reflect the participant’s
22 preference for a diet that involves protein sources that are exclusively, predominantly or partially
23 plant-based, and that every program provider serving congregate meals shall offer one or more
24 plant-based protein sources at every meal the program provider serves.
25 SECTION 4. Section 65 of chapter 23A of the General Laws, as so appearing, is hereby
26 amended, in subsection (a), by inserting after the word “options” the following words:-
27 “, including plant-based protein sources,”
28 SECTION 5. Said section 65 of said chapter 23A is hereby further amended by striking
29 subsection (d) and inserting in place thereof the following subsection:-
30 (d) The community development financial institution shall work in conjunction with
31 local community colleges and vocational education institutions to develop programs designed to
32 train and educate food sellers respecting the designating and marketing of nutritious foods,
33 including plant-based protein sources, and to assist in the development of public education
2 of 5
34 programs designed to make the buying public aware of the need for nutritious foods and the
35 identity of nutritious foods, including the health, climate, and environmental benefits of
36 maintaining a diet that involves protein sources that are exclusively, predominantly or partially
37 plant-based.
38 SECTION 6. Said section 65 of said chapter 23A is hereby further amended, in
39 subsection (h), by inserting after the word “food” the following words:-
40 “, including plant-based protein sources,”
41 SECTION 7. Chapter 69 of the General Laws is hereby amended by inserting the
42 following new section:-
43 Section 38. (a) The Office for Food and Nutrition Programs within the department shall
44 offer, on at least an annual basis, voluntary training and resources concerning plant-based protein
45 sources to schools and other organizations participating in the National School Lunch Program
46 or the Child Adult Food Care Program. Said training and resources shall provide information
47 including but not be limited to: (i) the health, climate, and environmental benefits of maintaining
48 a diet that involves protein sources that are exclusively, predominantly or partially plant-based;
49 and (ii) how to integrate plant-based protein sources into a healthy diet and prepare healthy
50 meals that involve protein sources that are exclusively, predominantly or partially plant-based.
51 (b) In awarding all grants, including but not limited to the School Nutrition Equipment
52 Assistance Grant for Schools, MA Farming Reinforces Education and Student Health, and the
53 Summer Food Service Program, said Office for Food and Nutrition Programs shall give positive
54 weight to applications that will: (i) increase access to meals involving plant-based protein
55 sources; or (ii) increase awareness of the health, climate, and environmental benefits of
3 of 5
56 maintaining a diet that involves protein sources that are exclusively, predominantly or partially
57 plant-based.
58 SECTION 8. Chapter 74 of the General Laws is hereby amended by inserting the
59 following new section:-
60 Section 59. All culinary arts programs approved by the commissioner pursuant to section
61 2 of this chapter shall provide instruction concerning: (i) the health, climate, and environmental
62 benefits of maintaining a diet that involves protein sources that are exclusively, predominantly or
63 partially plant-based; and (ii) how to integrate plant-based protein sources into a healthy diet and
64 prepare healthy meals that involve protein sources that are exclusively, predominantly or
65 partially plant-based.
66 SECTION 9. Section 5 of chapter 111I of the General Laws, as appearing in the 2022
67 Official Edition, is hereby amended by striking out the second paragraph and inserting in place
68 thereof the following paragraph:-
69 A monthly food package shall be provided to each participant and shall be prescribed by
70 a competent professional authority at the local WIC program based solely on the participant's
71 dietary needs; provided that in prescribing such monthly food package, the competent
72 professional authority shall consider the health benefits of maintaining a diet that involves
73 protein sources that are exclusively, predominantly or partially plant-based. Such competent
74 professional authorities shall be allowed to choose from the full range of types and amounts of
75 food authorized by federal rules in developing food packages for program participants. Program
76 participants shall have the right to request and receive a food package that reflects the
4 of 5
77 participant’s preference for a diet that involves protein sources that are exclusively,
78 predominantly or partially plant-based.
79 SECTION 10. The department of elementary and secondary education shall, by not later
80 than July 1, 2027, update the Comprehensive Health and Physical Education Framework to
81 include standards concerning: (i) how to maintain a healthy diet that involves protein sources that
82 are exclusively, predominantly or partially plant-based; (ii) the health, climate, and
83 environmental benefits of maintaining a diet that involves protein sources that are exclusively,
84 predominantly or partially plant-based.
